
## OWNER'S PREFACE (not for submission — for the author)

**The whole argument in four moves.**

1. *The theorem (cited, not ours).* Lela (2026) proves: if a branch-weight
   depends only on a branch's internally visible refinement structure
   (Condition 1), and if branches can be split in every proportion
   (Condition 2), the only consistent non-negative weight is
   amplitude-squared. The exponent comes from the Pythagorean theorem.
2. *Splitting in every proportion (our Theorem 1).* A tilted quantum coin —
   a spare ancilla, rotated by θ, then decohered — splits any branch in any
   ratio without disturbing the branch's own record. We verify this device
   against the theorem's definitions, clause by clause.
3. *Weight sees only internal structure (our §§6–7).* Decoherence seals a
   branch's record-life off from its siblings and from might-have-been
   experiments. A weight tracking the sealed-off stuff would track what no
   possible evidence could reflect. If rational expectation must answer to
   possible evidence — weaker than Elga's indifference principle — weight
   supervenes on internal structure. That is Condition 1, derived.
4. *The residue, faced (our §§5, 8).* The coin-tilts of move 2 are
   alternatives; demanding one weight rule across all of them is
   non-contextuality in disguise. Everettian answer: delegate the tilt
   choice to a quantum coin and every tilt happens, in sibling branches of
   you. Consistency across contexts becomes consistency across your own
   actual futures.

**What we still assume** (the ledger, §12): evidence-answerability (EI);
the Hilbert norm as the dynamics' own yardstick (NG); that self-locating
credence is well-posed (inherited from BMSL §14.6); plus three local,
defused premises (Ancilla Availability; the functional reading of
"readable"; delegation-availability). The exponent 2 is assumed nowhere.
