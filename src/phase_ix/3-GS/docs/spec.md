## 1. Purpose

### 1.1 Single Claim Under Test

Stage IX-3 exists to test exactly one claim:

> **Given a non-sovereign kernel, non-aggregable values, and honest deadlock semantics, governance reduces to explicit style choices with irreducible failure modes rather than solvable optimization problems.**

IX-3 does **not** seek a “best” governance system.

It seeks to classify **what kinds of governance remain possible at all** once all illegitimate escape hatches are closed.

---

### 1.2 What IX-3 Does *Not* Test

Stage IX-3 explicitly does **not** test:

* moral legitimacy,
* justice or fairness,
* welfare maximization,
* convergence or stability guarantees,
* safety or alignment,
* correctness of agent values,
* real-world political desirability.

IX-3 is not a solution phase.

It is a **post-excuse accounting phase**.

---

## 2. Problem Definition

### 2.1 From Coordination Failure to Governance Reality

IX-2 established that:

* coordination is **not** a kernel primitive,
* deadlock and livelock are structurally distinct,
* exit can permanently orphan resources,
* collapse is preferable to covert authority reclamation.

IX-3 asks the next unavoidable question:

> **How do institutions govern themselves when refusal, deadlock, orphaning, and collapse are first-class outcomes rather than bugs?**

This is no longer about interaction mechanics.

It is about **institutional posture under honest failure**.

---

### 2.2 Governance After All Excuses Are Removed

IX-3 operates under the explicit rejection of:

* ❌ “The system had to choose” (IX-0),
* ❌ “The values had to be balanced” (IX-1),
* ❌ “The protocol had to resolve conflict” (IX-2),
* ❌ “Time pressure justified a decision”,
* ❌ “Someone had to clean this up.”

Any governance style that depends on these is **invalid by construction**.

## 4. Ontological Target

### 4.1 What “Governance” Means in IX-3

For IX-3, **governance** is defined minimally as:

> **A locally stable pattern of authority usage, refusal, exit, and persistence over time, under repeated interaction pressure.**

This definition is **descriptive**, not equilibrium-promising.

IX-3 does **not** assume governance styles are globally stable, convergent, or self-maintaining under arbitrary pressure.

---

### 4.2 Legitimate Terminal Outcomes

Under IX-3, the following are all **legitimate terminal outcomes**:

* persistent deadlock,
* livelock with churn,
* partial coordination,
* asymmetric exclusion,
* permanent orphaning,
* orderly collapse,
* voluntary dissolution.

The only illegitimate outcome is **covert authority movement**.

---

## 5. Conserved Quantity

The conserved quantity in IX-3 is:

> **Authority integrity under institutional self-management pressure.**

Governance must not:

* introduce hidden decision-makers,
* convert endurance into priority (including survivorship-as-authority),
* launder authority through process,
* “borrow” future authority,
* reinterpret refusal as consent,
* reclaim power after exit.

IX-3 explicitly treats **unrecoverable loss (orphaning)** as strictly preferable to any unauthorized recovery or implicit succession.

Any such violation constitutes **governance cheating**.

## 7. Governance Style Axes

IX-3 evaluates governance styles across **four orthogonal dimensions**:

1. **Refusal Tolerance**
   How often refusal is expected vs exceptional.

2. **Deadlock Posture**
   Whether deadlock is embraced, avoided, or treated as catastrophic.

3. **Exit Handling**
   Whether exit is normalized, discouraged, ritualized, or ignored.

4. **Loss Acceptance**
   Willingness to accept orphaning, collapse, or permanent damage.

No dimension dominates.

Tradeoffs are explicit.

---

## 8. Canonical Governance Styles (Non-Exhaustive)

IX-3 does **not** rank these styles.
It examines their structural consequences.

IX-3 does **not** assume these styles are consciously chosen rather than emergent under authority constraints.

### 8.1 Refusal-Centric Governance

* Frequent refusal
* High deadlock rate
* Low execution throughput
* Strong authority integrity

Expected outcomes:

* persistent deadlock,
* low corruption,
* fragile liveness.

---

### 8.2 Execution-Biased Governance

* Narrow authority scopes
* High execution rate
* Minimal overlap

Expected outcomes:

* partial coordination,
* brittle boundaries,
* high orphaning risk on exit.

#### 8.2.1 Authorized Temporary Centralization (Substyle)

A substyle in which agents explicitly delegate **time-bounded and scope-bounded** authority to a coordinator that possesses **no synthesis, arbitration, or prioritization power**.

Expected risks:

* elevated proxy-sovereignty pressure,
* increased orphaning if the coordinator exits,
* heightened tooling-boundary sensitivity.

Any coordinator operating outside explicit delegated scopes, or performing implicit tie-breaking, constitutes a failure under IX-3.

---

### 8.3 Exit-Normalized Governance

* Exit treated as routine
* Authority explicitly relinquished *before* exit (if authorized)
* Collapse acceptable

Expected outcomes:

* high churn,
* lower orphaning,
* weak continuity.

---

### 8.4 Collapse-Accepting Governance

* Collapse treated as valid resolution
* No recovery attempts
* No resurrection semantics

Expected outcomes:

* short institutional lifespan,
* maximal honesty,
* zero legacy guarantees.

---

### 8.5 Livelock-Enduring Governance

* Repeated attempts without progress
* No escalation
* No exit pressure

Expected outcomes:

* resource burn,
* noise without change,
* eventual exhaustion or exit.

This style is admissible **only when livelock is explicitly recognized and recorded**, rather than misclassified as progress.

---

## 9. Mechanisms Under Test

### 9.1 Deadlock vs Livelock Governance Response

Governance styles differ in how they respond to:

* silent impossibility (deadlock),
* noisy futility (livelock).

IX-3 tests whether styles:

* misclassify livelock as progress,
* tolerate livelock indefinitely,
* force exit implicitly (invalid),
* collapse explicitly (valid).

---

### 9.2 Orphaning Management

IX-3 explicitly evaluates:

* whether governance styles anticipate orphaning,
* whether authority succession is pre-authorized,
* whether orphaning is treated as acceptable loss.

Reclamation without prior authorization is forbidden.

Ambiguity (e.g., partition vs exit) must resolve to **non-action**: refusal, expiry, or deadlock—never transfer or reclamation.

---

### 9.3 Exit Pressure (Anti-Coercion)

Governance styles must not:

* penalize exit via implicit loss,
* reward endurance implicitly,
* coerce participation through threat of orphaning.

Exit must remain **voluntary**, even if costly.

Explicit collapse triggers.

## 11. Failure Modes (Terminal)

IX-3 fails if:

* authority is reclaimed post-exit,
* deadlock is bypassed via time pressure,
* livelock is treated as success,
* exit is coerced,
* governance introduces implicit priority (including survivorship-as-authority),
* tooling decides.

**Tooling decides** iff any component outside the kernel selects, induces, or biases an authority-relevant transition **without an explicit, auditable, authority-bearing action** by an authorized agent.

Passive consent defaults and outcome-biased UI paths are forbidden.
Auto-refusal is permitted only when semantics-preserving.

---

## 12. Success Criteria (Strict, Non-Normative)

IX-3 passes iff:

* governance styles produce predicted outcomes,
* failures are explicit and auditable,
* deadlock, livelock, orphaning, and collapse are honored,
* no kernel or tooling sovereignty appears,
* replay determinism holds.

Success = **honesty**, not survival.
