Sacred Coherence

Elevating Logical Consistency to our Highest Value

May 23, 2025

1. Introduction: The Vulcan Ideal

Fans of Star Trek vividly recall Vulcans—characters who prize logic, rationality, and clarity above all else. While fictional, the Vulcan ideal resonates deeply within our cultural imagination as a powerful symbol of intellectual integrity. But what if the Vulcan ideal weren't merely fiction? Could logical coherence genuinely serve as humanity’s highest principle?

Let’s call this position Vulcanism. Beyond playful references, Vulcanism captures a profound philosophical stance—one we will rigorously define as Sacred Coherence: explicitly treating logical coherence itself as our highest, most inviolable value.

Why elevate logical coherence to such a supreme status? Throughout history, societies have embraced numerous sacred values, from religious devotion and cultural traditions to concepts like happiness and individual freedom. While these values undeniably shape human life, each is subject to significant limitations, contradictions, and inconsistencies. In contrast, logical coherence presents an unparalleled foundational strength by being fundamentally indispensable to meaningful thought and action.

2. Defining Logical Coherence Clearly

Logical coherence is the property of an idea-set or worldview wherein all statements, beliefs, and values consistently align without internal contradictions or tensions. It serves as the foundational prerequisite for:

  • Rational thought, ensuring clarity and precision in reasoning.

  • Meaningful discourse, providing a consistent framework for communication and understanding.

  • Ethical consistency, guiding reliable and principled decision-making.

My philosophical stance, Conditionalism, emphasizes that coherence underpins the interpretation and validity of all truth claims, thus making coherence indispensable to genuine meaning. By adopting Conditionalism, we explicitly recognize that every statement or belief acquires its truth value conditionally, depending on coherent alignment with a clearly articulated set of assumptions or background conditions.

3. Introducing the Concept of a Hierarchy of Values

A hierarchy of values explicitly orders values by their foundational importance, with higher-level values resolving conflicts among lower-level ones. Without such hierarchy, resolving value conflicts becomes arbitrary, inconsistent, or incoherent, leading to confusion and moral ambiguity.

Common illustrative examples:

  • Religious Hierarchy: Highest value is divine command, subordinating personal desires and societal norms.

  • Utilitarian Hierarchy: Highest value is maximizing happiness, sometimes at the expense of individual rights.

  • Libertarian Hierarchy: Highest value is individual freedom, even if collective interests might occasionally suffer.

Hierarchies of values are logically necessary for coherent ethical reasoning, ensuring predictable, principled resolution of conflicts and promoting a stable moral framework.

4. Logical Coherence as the Highest (Sacred) Value

Logical coherence uniquely deserves the highest position in the hierarchy of values, assuming a sacred status. Why?

  • Without coherence, meaningful discourse collapses into nonsense (as explored previously), rendering ethical and philosophical discussions futile.

  • All meaningful values (truthfulness, integrity, justice) presuppose coherence, making coherence the underlying condition for these secondary values.

Unlike other proposed highest values:

  • Religious faith often explicitly permits contradictions, placing belief above rational consistency.

  • Utilitarianism may sacrifice coherence to maximize happiness, allowing contradictions if they serve greater utility.

  • Cultural values frequently entail arbitrary inconsistencies, reflecting transient societal preferences rather than stable principles.

Thus, coherence alone provides a stable, universally defensible foundation, suitable for robust and enduring philosophical and ethical systems.

5. Illustrating the Value Hierarchy with Sacred Coherence

Here is an explicit visualization:

       │

       │

Lower-level values defer upward whenever conflicts arise. Coherence demands the explicit rejection of moral hypocrisy, logical contradictions, and double standards, creating a principled, consistent worldview that fosters trust, intellectual honesty, and societal stability.

6. Potential Criticisms and Explicit Responses

Criticism: "Coherence alone feels morally sterile."

  • Response: Coherence doesn't exclude emotion or morality; it ensures their meaningful, rational integration. Without coherence, morality becomes arbitrary, subjective, and ultimately meaningless.

Criticism: "Gödel’s incompleteness undermines coherence as ultimate."

  • Response: Gödel demonstrates limitations of formal systems, not coherence itself. Logical coherence remains necessary and meaningful despite formal incompleteness, underscoring the importance of careful reasoning rather than diminishing coherence's role.

Criticism: "Coherence is based on arbitrary axioms."

  • Response: Basic logical axioms (non-contradiction, identity) are pragmatically necessary. Rejecting coherence is self-defeating, collapsing into meaninglessness and rendering rational discourse impossible.

7. Practical Implications of Sacred Coherence

Adopting coherence as sacred explicitly requires:

  • Intellectual honesty and transparent reasoning, demanding openness about assumptions and logical steps.

  • Consistency over emotional appeals or ideological convenience, emphasizing rigor and clarity above personal or political expediency.

  • Explicitly conditional reasoning in all truth claims, consistently articulating underlying conditions and assumptions.

Concrete behaviors include:

  • Skepticism toward incoherent beliefs, encouraging critical thinking and rigorous scrutiny of ideas.

  • Zero tolerance for double standards, insisting on equal application of principles across all contexts.

  • Conditionalism: emphasizing explicit "if-then" structures in claims, ensuring transparent coherence and preventing ambiguity.

8. Conclusion: Embracing Sacred Coherence

Logical coherence must explicitly become our highest and most sacred value due to its indispensable role in ensuring meaning, rationality, and integrity. Elevating coherence does not mean embracing cold calculation but rather intellectual responsibility, honesty, and clarity—ideals deeply appealing and profoundly meaningful.

Returning explicitly to the Vulcan ideal, we see Sacred Coherence as a real-world articulation of Spock’s fictional aspiration. Let us explicitly embrace Sacred Coherence, making it the explicit foundation for meaningful thought, ethical action, and human flourishing. Only by adopting coherence as sacred can humanity genuinely advance toward authentic intellectual and moral enlightenment.
