The Law of Control Work

In the previous post, we introduced the kybit, a fundamental measure of intentional control over future outcomes. Now we formally establish the physical principle governing the energetic cost of exercising such control—the Law of Control Work:

Exercising intentional control over future outcomes requires physical work proportional to the kybits of control exerted.

Significance of This Law

Just as erasing information has a known thermodynamic cost (Landauer's Principle), intentionally shaping future outcomes—exercising agency—also incurs a measurable thermodynamic cost.

Agency, therefore, is never free.

Each deliberate action undertaken to influence future possibilities involves consuming real physical resources, fundamentally embedding agency within physical reality.

Drift vs. Control: Energy Landscapes

  • Drift represents the spontaneous, entropic evolution of systems toward disorder, which occurs without intentional effort.

  • Control requires intentional energy investment to move against entropy and selectively amplify specific futures.

The greater the degree of intentional control (the more kybits exerted), the greater the thermodynamic cost.

Real-World Illustrations

Clear examples of genuine agency include:

  • An AI-controlled drone adjusting its flight path in real-time based on predictive simulations to avoid obstacles.

  • An investment portfolio managed by a predictive algorithm continuously adjusting holdings to maximize future returns.

  • An animal storing food based on environmental cues predicting future scarcity.

In each of these scenarios, agents utilize predictive internal models and expend physical energy to intentionally shape future outcomes, clearly exemplifying the principles of agency.
