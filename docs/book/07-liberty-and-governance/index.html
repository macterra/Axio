Volume 7 — Liberty, Coercion, and Governance

Design (Phase 2, 2026-07-08). 35 chapters in seven parts from 94 posts (May 2025–Jul 2026) — the largest roster, no split per author decision. Most posts are short single-argument pieces; chapters merge two to five of them. The organizing editorial fact, found independently in every cluster: the corpus matures from categorical polemic (mid-2025) to conditional, structural argument (2026). The book writes every chapter from the mature register — the Grey Zone for coercion legitimacy, reciprocity for rights, the causal ladder for the speech/violence boundary, the dose/domain taxonomy for socialism, and the May/July 2026 Axiocracy statements — with the earlier polemics demoted to illustrative voice.

Chapter plan

Part I — Coercion and the State

  1. The Individualist Premise — the individual as the moral unit; the axis orthogonal to left–right
  2. Upgrading Liberty — Mill’s harm principle → NAP → the Agency Protection Principle; agency as the political goal
  3. The Coercion Continuum — taxation and slavery differ in degree; statism’s structural license; partial ownership
  4. Extortion-Funded Organizations — the funding test: VFO/EFO/RFO; parasitism, plunder, hidden monetary extraction
  5. The Grey Zone — the mature position: coercion’s legitimacy is conditional, provisional, burden-scaled

Part II — Rights

  1. Rights Are Forged — and Reciprocal — power makes boundaries real; reciprocity makes them legitimate
  2. Needs, Conflicts, and Hard Cases — negative rights only; rights collide; freedom of association

Part III — Free Speech

  1. The Listener’s Right — censorship as coercion against the audience; thought and speech inseparable
  2. Where Speech Ends — Popper’s paradox → the causal ladder; incitement as delegated violence
  3. The Censorship Machine — how liberal societies drift into cognitive regulation
  4. Civilizational Infrastructure — the architecture of a free-speech order (capstone)

Part IV — Justice and Law

  1. Two Kinds of Law — common law resolves conflict; command law manufactures it
  2. Law and Power — law binds only where power is balanced; governed by the tails
  3. The Epistemics of Guilt — presumption of innocence as institutionalized humility; adjudication redesigned
  4. Restitution, Not Retribution — Universal Compensatory Justice; the limits of enforcement
  5. Borders and Movement — immigration, cultural threat, colonization, and justice after it

Part V — The Egalitarian Temptation

  1. Agency, Not Equality — poverty is the problem; luck doesn’t justify coercion; equal opportunity is incoherent
  2. The Corruption of Compassion — coercive redistribution, UBI, and aid that funds oppressors
  3. Against Envy — the invalid inference from disparity to injury; mobility, not snapshots
  4. Against Socialism — need is not title; the dose/domain/monopoly/exit/feedback taxonomy
  5. Against Communism — the machine of maximum immiseration; why family communism doesn’t scale
  6. Against Utopia — closure, civilizational optimization, and the Sacrifice Pattern

Part VI — Political Pathologies

  1. The Authoritarian Default — the psychology, dynamics, and definition of the recurring temptation
  2. Why Liberalism Loses — the structural unpopularity of liberty; permanent adolescence
  3. Fictions of the Collective — nations are not agents; interests are not primitives
  4. Words That Lost Their Teeth — three socialisms; Doyle’s razor; semantic hygiene
  5. Centrism as a Discipline — governance under uncertainty and irreversibility (Part VI capstone)

Part VII — Order Without the State

  1. The Conversion of Coercion — politics as exploit; civilization as the replacement of politics with protocols
  2. The Archist Axiom — coercion does not require monopoly; the hardest public-goods case
  3. Anarchy Is Not Chaos — voluntary hierarchy with exit; the international system as working anarchy
  4. The Hardest Case: Defense — protection without sovereignty; the two-way burden of proof
  5. Exit to Protocol — the state as obsolete technology; legitimacy migrating to voluntary systems
  6. The Limits of Leviathan — why scale destroys evaluability; Dominions as the digital limit case
  7. The Admissible Region — politics as phase space; inside or outside where agency remains viable
  8. Axiocracy — governance by value discovery; the Mars constitution (volume capstone)

Design decisions

  • Latest-position discipline, cluster by cluster. Coercion: “always authoritarian” (Aug 2025) yields to the Grey Zone’s conditional legitimacy (May 2026). Rights: Rights Are Reciprocal (Jun 2026) explicitly repairs Rights Are Forged (“Slaveholders had preferences”). Speech: the Dec 2025 causal ladder (Tier 1 expression / Tier 2 epistemic violence / Tier 3 delegated violence) supersedes both the Sep maximalism and Brandenburg imminence; the Jun 2026 capstone narrows platform enforcement to conduct-only — the book states this two-level structure (state/legal theory vs platform rule) explicitly. Redistribution: “always harmful” absolutism (Jun 2025) matures into Against Socialism’s dose/domain/monopoly/exit/feedback taxonomy (Jun 2026). Axiocracy: May 2026 for the definition (demonstrated exchange value), Jul 2026 for the institutions (axiocratic constitutionalism, engineered exit), Oct 2025 surviving as lineage and aphorism stock.
  • Definitions cite Volume 5. Coercion (“credible threat of actual harm to gain compliance”), harm, consent are canonical in Vol 5 (What Counts as Coercion, The Boundaries of Force) — every chapter cites rather than re-derives. Harm language standardizes on the mature formulation: contraction of reachable futures; enforced impossibility, not difficulty.
  • Terminology reconciliations. Incitement: the Aug 2025 post says incitement is not coercion; the Dec 2025 ladder classifies incitement to violence as Tier 3 delegated violence — a third category, neither protected speech nor coercion; ch 9 harmonizes. APP’s biconditional (coercion justified iff it prevents/remedies agency violations) vs Upgrading Liberty’s ΔNetAgency > 0 comparator are different rules — ch 2 reconciles (APP as the constraint, net-agency as the comparator within it) — AUTHOR FLAG to confirm.
  • Cross-volume seams. Vol 4 ch 12 owns the formal results (Sacrifice–Collapse, Leviathan/coalitional robustness, Dominions papers); Vol 7 carries the popular statements with explicit citations (chs 22, 33). Vol 6 owns economics (markets, minimum wage, Bitcoin mechanics, inflation mechanics). Vol 8 owns Cancel Culture and Algorithmic Corruption of Language — chs 10/11 link, don’t absorb. Vol 3 ch 33 (Politics of Safety) cross-linked from ch 27.
  • Roster. All 94 posts placed; no cuts. how-to-lose-an-election is mined for one paragraph only (ch 24). introducing-larpitarianism survives as a sidebar in ch 26. Posts reassigned across parts per reader findings: decriminalizing-discrimination → ch 7 (association, not speech); the-mirage-of-equilibrium → ch 10 (speech factions, not equality); the-fire-and-the-anvil → ch 6 (rights ontology); the-twilight-of-authority → ch 32 (legitimacy migration); law-and-order → ch 12.
  • Assets. ~20 content-bearing tweet screenshots (mostly transcribe as text per STYLE.md); four rebuildable figures/tables: the political triangle (docs/images/d924f1a0-…718x520.png), the three-socialisms table (71e28aa1-…756x488.png), the governance replacement table (71f4d793-…816x440.png), the taxation-enslavement spectrum (f810dae4-…3140x2641 — .bin repaired to .png), plus the Maximum Immiseration infographic (081f167c-…1055x1491.png, transcribed). No LaTeX anywhere in the roster.
