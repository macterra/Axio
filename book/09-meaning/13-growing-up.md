**Thesis.** "Grow up," said to a believer, is developmentally accurate rather than merely rude: maturity is the movement from Kegan's Socialized Mind (Stage 3, morality borrowed from the group) through the Self-Authoring Mind (Stage 4, values chosen and owned) toward the Self-Transforming Mind (Stage 5) — the enlightened heretic who re-authors an inherited faith into chosen values rather than discarding it. Institutions conspire to hold people at Stage 3 because obedience scales and growth is metabolically expensive. Maturity does not repudiate inheritance; it re-authors it — which is exactly what a letter to one's own ancestors performs: honoring their best aspirations (liberty, curiosity, fairness) while revising the rest (inherited authority, hierarchy), on the principle that legitimacy is continuously earned, never automatic. "Faith is how we first borrow morality; maturity is how we later earn it."
