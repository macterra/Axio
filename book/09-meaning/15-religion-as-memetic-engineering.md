**Thesis.** Religion is best understood not by its metaphysical claims but by its function: a memeplex evolved to deliver meaning, surviving by fitness rather than truth — "a religion that does not confer meaning simply dies out." Meaning is its adaptive niche: narrative for chaos, a reason to act under uncertainty, binding ritual, a moral scaffold, a vocabulary of purpose and identity. And what evolved blindly can be engineered deliberately. The Church of Virus was exactly that attempt — a self-aware, rational religion that keeps religion's functional strengths while shedding its epistemic liabilities: virtues (reason, empathy, vision) as memetic attractors, "sins" (faith, apathy, hypocrisy) as cognitive antibodies, a memetic immune system that exposes its own architecture where traditional religions obscure theirs. Meaning no longer has to be inherited; it can be engineered.
