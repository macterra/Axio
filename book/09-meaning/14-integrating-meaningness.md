**Thesis.** Three thinkers map the same order-versus-flux polarity — Peterson's Order/Chaos (mythic), Chapman's Form/Nebulosity (phenomenological), Axio's Coherence/Chaos (information-theoretic) — and the comparison sets up the synthesis. The mature move is not to choose among them but, in the dialectic's oldest sense, to transcend and include: Chapman's Meaningness and Axio are dual aspects of one dynamical loop. Meaningness supplies the phenomenology — how meaning feels, surfaces, and is participated in ("the how without the why"); Axio supplies the mechanics — the structural requirements of agency ("the why without the how"). Agency requires participation (sensory coupling, feedback); participation requires agency (selective attention, intentional arcs); the divide collapses. Meaning is the participatory dynamic through which agents cohere; value is the structural dynamic through which coherence propagates. There is no cosmic scoreboard — nihilism and eternalism are twin evasions — and meaning is generated in the transition from chaos to coherence, neither imposed nor discovered.
