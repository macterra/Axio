**Thesis.** The book's last word — a letter to the reader, and above all to the reader who still believes. The whole project's questioning of God has been method, not mockery: "When I question God, it is not mockery; it is method. My project is not to extinguish the sacred but to locate its lawful origin." Naturalism does not destroy the sacred; it redeems it, by finding where transcendence lawfully comes from — an emergent expression of nature rather than an intrusion into it. The reality of the experiences is granted in full — the unity, the awe, the sense of presence; only their supernatural *explanation* is set aside, and the account offered in its place (consciousness as what the universe feels like when it models itself, the collapse of observer and observed) is what the traditions were reaching for all along. The address is irenic and open: you are welcome here even if you believe, because "if there is a God worth reverence, it will not fear examination."

**Sources.** letter-to-the-faithful-reader (Nov 2025), standalone — the reconciliation of naturalism and the sacred; the self-modeling account of the divine (consciousness as the universe modeling itself); the coherence criterion as fidelity, not contempt; the warm, pastoral, second-person register.
