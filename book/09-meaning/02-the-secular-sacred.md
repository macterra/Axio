**Thesis.** Secular people have not outgrown the sacred; they have only changed its wardrobe. The sacred is not a supernatural category but a structural one: the non-negotiable apex of a value hierarchy — the value refused even under maximal strain, the thing that adjudicates all other conflicts. Everyone has one, explicit or hidden (autonomy, equality, nation, safety, progress, each sacred to someone), and pretending you have none does not liberate you; it blinds you, and leaves your hierarchy open to capture. Three naturalistic accounts map the sacred at three levels: social (Hanson — sacralization as coalition glue, kept vague to enable affirmation), phenomenological (Chapman — sacredness as an interactive mode available through wonder, ritual, attentiveness), and architectonic (Axio — the sacred is simply the top position that arbitrates value conflicts).
