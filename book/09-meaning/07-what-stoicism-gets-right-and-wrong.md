**Thesis.** A two-sided treatment, critique then recovery. Even the sophisticated three-discipline reading of Stoicism (desire, action, assent — via Gregory Lopez) recovers its ethical depth beyond the freshman "dichotomy of control," but Stoicism remains a fundamentally *defensive* philosophy: it excels at equanimity and emotional regulation and under-serves the expressive, creative, passionate dimensions of a flourishing life — and its objective value plus cosmic determinism sit uneasily with Conditionalism, subjective value, and a branching universe. Its degenerate form is the "resilient asshole," toughness without empathy. Yet five insights survive and integrate cleanly: the boundary of control, cognitive reframing, intentional reflective action, pro-social virtue (the expanding moral circle, against the detachment stereotype), and moment-to-moment discipline. Keep the tools; hand the missing dimension — passion, aesthetics, vitality — to Phosphorism.
