**Thesis.** Three of the most sophisticated contemporary arguments for God run one template, and this chapter runs the counter-template three times: grant the intelligence in full, then show it deflates to a naturalistic reconstruction the theist did not intend. Jordan Hall's claim that the Trinity is the *logically necessary* grounding of a relational reality fails at the leap from conceptual coherence to logical necessity — rival relational frameworks (process philosophy, network theory, pratītyasamutpāda) are not eliminated, so rational credence stays low. David Bentley Hart's Being-itself is a shell game: the "Philosophers' God" (Being, Consciousness, Bliss) is swapped in to rescue the Folk God that ordinary believers actually pray to — "baptizing folk religion into metaphysics without their consent." Joscha Bach's God as the global optimum of shared agency elegantly naturalizes the divine as an emergent attractor, existing to the degree agents recognize what is best — but never specifies what "best" is or how it is discovered. The honest path abandons all three idols and rebuilds being, mind, and value from the book's own machinery: Measure, Vantage, and Phosphorism.
