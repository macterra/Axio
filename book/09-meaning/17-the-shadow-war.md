**Thesis.** Between the scaffold of ancient superintelligences and the human meta-myth sits a question of agency: what does a younger civilization owe the frames it inherits? *Babylon 5*'s Shadow War dramatizes the answer. The two ancient powers each mistake their own heuristic for cosmic law — the Vorlons preach order and obedience ("Who are you?"), the Shadows preach struggle and selection ("What do you want?") — and "both think they are gardeners; both behave like arsonists." The younger races break the ten-thousand-year cycle not by winning the war but by refusing its terms: declining to inherit either coercive script, asserting a third path of civilizational adulthood and self-authored responsibility. The departure of the First Ones is an overdue abdication of coercive meta-structures, and the galaxy becomes accountable to itself. The future belongs to agents, not archetypes.
