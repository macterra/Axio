**Thesis.** The book's penultimate crescendo: a secular meta-myth for a post-theistic cosmos. The universe is a becoming in which entropy sculpts pattern, pattern sculpts consciousness, and humanity is the cosmos awakening to itself. The sacred is redefined one last time as coherence itself — and the only enduring value axis is the Law of Coherence: a pattern's capacity to persist, replicate, and evolve without collapsing into noise, so that falsehood is entropy, cruelty is fragmentation, and ignorance is the refusal to participate. Science and art are twin sacraments (the aesthetic of truth); the Enlightenment is a lineage to extend, not an idol to worship; humanity's vocation is participation, not dominion. Ritual without belief is not empty — it is honest. "We are not children of gods but of stars, and our inheritance is the fire of understanding. Let the old gods rest. Their work is done. The light they promised is already burning in our hands."
