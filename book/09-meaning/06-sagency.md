**Thesis.** Agency is power without guidance — the capacity to act, morally neutral, as capable of destruction as of creation. Sagency (sage + agency) is agency disciplined by wisdom: the synthesis that turns raw capability into stewardship. It is the product, not the sum, of three factors — comprehension (seeing what is), discernment (judging what matters), and embodiment (acting effectively) — and because they multiply, the absence of any one zeroes the whole: comprehension without embodiment is impotence, embodiment without discernment is recklessness, discernment without comprehension is illusion. The sagent pursues coherence rather than goals, values timing and restraint, and holds an active equilibrium. "Power without wisdom is destruction. Wisdom without power is irrelevance. Sagency is their synthesis."
