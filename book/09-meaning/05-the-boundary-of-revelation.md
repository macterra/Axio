**Thesis.** If the sacred is to be kept without the supernatural, a boundary is needed: which inner experiences carry knowledge and which only feel as though they do. Subjective intensity is not evidence of metaphysical reality. A psychedelic revelation of cosmic oneness would have to clear five epistemic bars — intersubjective verifiability, novel predictive power, internal consistency, cultural independence, objective informational gain — and none on record does; the experiences reveal genuine truths about brain, perception, and psychology, but not about reality itself. Dreams are the mirror case done fairly: against the claim that they are "just random neural firing" and therefore meaningless, random inputs to a pattern-making filter still yield meaningful outputs about the filter — dreams carry genuine individual meaning (the mind organizing its own noise), just not the universal symbolism of the dream dictionary. Meaning is emergent coherence the mind finds, not a message decoded from elsewhere.
