**Thesis.** The "clown world" diagnosis — that a culture which has lost its absolutes has descended into nihilism, left-wing perpetual deconstruction and right-wing ironic will-to-power alike — names a real malaise but offers a false cure. Meaning does not require God, and the choice is not between inherited absolutes and a clownish void. There is a third option: recognize that values are contingent (Conditionalism — every truth, value, and meaning holds relative to background conditions) and then consciously affirm the ones worth having (Phosphorism — life, intelligence, complexity, authenticity). Contingency is not collapse; it makes meaning more deliberate, more accountable, more authentic. We do not inherit significance; we choose it, construct it, and defend it. The stance is neither eternalist (meaning fixed from outside) nor nihilist (no meaning at all) but the active equilibrium between them — a true neutral that is agile navigation, not detachment.
