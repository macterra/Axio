**Thesis.** The terse wisdom formulas a culture hands down — a mother's "look where you're going," the schoolyard "mind your own business," the maxims carved at Delphi — unfold, read carefully, into disciplines of attention, boundary, and measured agency. "Look where you're going" ascends from spatial safety through attention management, trajectory, foresight, and consequence to the philosophical claim that where the mental gaze lands, action follows. "Mind your own business" runs the same ascent through psychological detachment, healthy boundaries, comparative advantage, anti-paternalism, and the personal origin of meaning. The three Delphic inscriptions form a triad of restraint — know thyself, nothing in excess, surety brings ruin — the guardrails without which freedom collapses into chaos. And the four-beat mantra (wake up, show up, level up, grow up) turns the same wisdom into a cyclical practice revisited at every life stage.
