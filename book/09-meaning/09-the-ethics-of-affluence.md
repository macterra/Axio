**Thesis.** Affluence destroys meaning only passively. Agency lives in the tension between constraint and possibility, so when every appetite can be indulged, choice collapses into compulsion — the annihilation of agency by abundance that *Infinite Jest* dramatizes. But affluence is an achievement, not a coffin; the danger is *passive* affluence, prosperity merely absorbed. The ethical response is neither to flee wealth nor to drown in it but to wield it: to turn abundance into the freedom to choose worthy struggles — chosen constraints, deliberate adversity. Discipline of body and mind, creation, service beyond the self, truth-seeking, meaningful risk: these are how a person keeps agency alive in conditions engineered to make it unnecessary.
