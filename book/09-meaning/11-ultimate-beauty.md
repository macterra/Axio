**Thesis.** Ask "why?" of any choice, and again of the answer, and again: the recursion of justification does not bottom out in logic or in ethics but in aesthetics — the felt sense of rightness, coherence, elegance, resonance. At the base of agency sits an aesthetic recognition that is terminal and self-justifying: "this is beautiful, and so I choose it." The circularity of root values is not a defect but the necessary structure of any value system — decision theory's utility functions, ethics' moral intuitions, and agency's preferences all descend to the same unjustifiable terminus, and the honest response is to name it rather than pretend a further reason waits below. This is why the creed of the previous chapter bottoms out where it does: the four pillars are affirmed by aesthetic recognition, not derived from a prior premise.
