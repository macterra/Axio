**Thesis.** Part IV opens with the metaphysical scaffold for a secular eschatology. Merge eternalism (the block universe: the future is as real as the present, fixed in the geometry of spacetime) with singularitarianism (minds vastly greater than ours are coming) and a startling picture follows: future superintelligences already exist, embedded in the manifold, "as real and permanent as any past civilization." Godhood, on this reading, is defined by the relative scale and coherence of a will, not by any supernatural attribute — a naturalistic polytheism, gods without the supernatural. The idea is held with discipline, not zeal: its own self-critiques are kept in view — existence in the block is not present agency (a category error to elide), treating the future as inevitable is epistemic overreach, and a polytheism of future minds carries a real value-alignment blind spot.
