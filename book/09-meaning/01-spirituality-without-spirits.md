**Thesis.** "Spirituality" is a linguistic fossil. Its root is animist — *spiritus*, breath, the vital wind, cognate with *pneuma*, *prāṇa*, *ruach* — and the literal ontology it named (an immaterial vital force that survives the body) is obsolete. Yet the word survives, and survives for a reason: it uniquely names the enduring human pursuit of transcendence — going beyond the ego, the grind, brute material existence. The ghost is gone; the scaffolding remains, because humans still need more than facts. The volume opens by keeping the hunger and discarding the haunting.

**Sources.** understanding-spirituality (Aug 2025): the six-stage genealogy — etymology (breath = life); hyper-agency detection breeding animism and its mediators; religious reification of spirit into immortal soul; the Enlightenment severing the literal ontology but not the need; modern spirituality as orientation toward transcendence (prayer, meditation, art, psychedelics, philosophy, science — united by orientation, not doctrine); the term persisting as vestige because no other word carries the aura.
