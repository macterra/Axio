# Reader-facing glossary audit

Status: **Phase 6 complete**

## Scope

The published glossary in `book/00-front/03-glossary.md` derives from the canonical terminology sheet after all eight Phase 5 reconciliation passes. It covers all 101 terminology headings, groups them alphabetically, and points each entry to its canonical defining chapter or chapter sequence. Selected entries also link to later applications where those materially clarify scope.

The glossary is a reader aid rather than a substitute for local definition. Entries compress the internal authority's constraints; the linked manuscript chapter governs the full argument.

## Publication decisions

- Retain every term whose book usage is narrower than ordinary usage or whose capitalization signals a term of art.
- Retain *Axion* because Volume 4 Chapter 11 defines it explicitly, while cross-linking it to the more general RSA and sovereignty entries.
- Resolve the older paper-glossary seams in favor of the edited manuscript: integration, reflection, and causal efficacy are not additional constitutive conditions of knowledge; unanimous community consent is not constitutive of property's practical authorization structure.
- Mark proposed metrics, conjectures, models, tests, frameworks, governance designs, and personal commitments so a coined name does not inflate epistemic status.
- Give prominent internal contrasts to Measure/Credence, agency/sovereignty, awareness/sentience/sapience, harm/coercion/force/violence, coherence/viability, semantic/existential meaning, function/purpose, and sacredness/normative endorsement.
- Link the first natural uses of the central vocabulary in the Preface and Introduction to glossary anchors. Canonical chapters continue to define their own terms locally.
