## Ownership after compression

- **Chapter 1 owns the premise:** individual persons are the primary moral unit, while group claims must be unpacked into affected agents and authorization relations.
- **Chapter 2 owns the gate:** the Agency Protection Principle is necessary but not sufficient, net agency compares only candidate responses, and the Grey Zone supplies the remaining justification burdens.
- **Chapter 3 owns the dimension:** taxation and slavery share a compelled-extraction axis without being morally equivalent; title, exit, bodily control, legal standing, and brutality remain separate.
- **Chapter 4 owns the funding mechanism:** VFO, EFO, and RFO classify how revenue arrives. Classification does not settle institutional legitimacy or the empirical parasite test.
- **Chapter 5 owns conditional legitimacy:** the Grey Zone procedure and its rising burden remain the controlling position of the volume.
- **Chapter 6 owns reciprocal rights:** moral claim, legal recognition, enforcement, reciprocity, the anti-evasion rule, protected domains, rights profiles, and non-monopoly enforcement.
- **Chapter 7 owns the consequences:** provision claims must identify a debtor and duty bridge; negative rights collide; association includes exclusion under market-power, duty, and public-accommodation qualifications.
