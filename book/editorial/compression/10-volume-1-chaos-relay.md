Date: July 21, 2026

## Selection audit

The remaining-sequence audit compared the pressure points already recorded for Volumes 1, 5, 6, and 8. Length was descriptive only and did not determine the selection.

- **Volume 1, Chapters 19–25 — selected.** Continuous reading confirmed that Chapters 20 and 23 restarted the emergence stack after adjacent chapters had already established its components. Chapter 20 previewed filtering, constructors, physics, life, and recursive self-modeling immediately before Chapter 21's canonical order. Chapter 23 then re-derived laws from Chaos before reaching its assigned subjects. This blurred inheritance and strengthened the speculative law-emergence claim beyond the surrounding qualifications.
- **Volume 5, Chapters 14–17 — no edit selected.** The rival-framework block has a shared anti-aggregation premise, but each chapter owns a different test: utilitarian aggregation and demandingness, moral extortion, nearby realist and egoist systems, and existence-boundary cases. The repeated premise is needed to expose different failure mechanisms rather than functioning as a restart.
- **Volume 6, Chapters 1–11 — no edit selected.** Chapters 1–5 distinguish scalar value, the value/wealth/capital/money taxonomy, money as ledger, price as transaction evidence, and attention as allocation. Chapter 6 is an explicit trial docket; Chapters 7–11 test different charges. Its previews and handoffs are visible, but the audit did not identify a bounded deletion that would preserve the comparative cases and qualifications.
- **Volume 8, Chapters 10–16 — no edit selected.** Continuous reading confirmed the Phase 4 ownership decision: bad-faith tactics, labels, temporal framing, victim currency, category corruption, enforcement, and virtue recruitment are distinct causal mechanisms. Their recurrent weapon-and-capture language provides the sequence's connective tissue; it does not substitute one mechanism for another.

The Volume 1 sequence therefore had the clearest demonstrated friction. The other three candidates remain available for the later cross-volume and line pass if sentence-level evidence emerges, but this audit creates no compression mandate for them.

- Chapter 20 now hands selection and interpretation to Chapter 21 instead of stating the complete emergence stack first. Its filter-code paragraph retains the interpreter and realization debts and passes them explicitly to constructors and life.
