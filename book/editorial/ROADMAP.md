# Axio Editorial Roadmap

*A dependency-first plan for turning the complete first draft into a coherent, rigorous, final manuscript.*

Status: **Phase 3 underway — Volume 1 edited and in review; Volume 2 next.** The manuscript contains nine drafted volumes, 240 chapters, and roughly 550,000 words. Phase 1 established the editorial control documents; Phase 2 confirmed the nine-volume architecture with targeted revisions recorded in `book/editorial/architecture/`. The first foundational-volume pass is recorded in `book/editorial/volume-edits/01-physics-of-agency.md`. Editing proceeds dependency-first through the remaining load-bearing volumes.

## Editorial goals

The edit should make *Axio*:

- More rigorous at the transitions between physics, philosophy, and ethics.
- Less repetitive when read sequentially without sacrificing volume independence.
- Clear about what is established, inferred, proposed, or speculative.
- Terminologically consistent across all nine volumes.
- Better calibrated in controversial and empirical passages.
- Shorter where compression strengthens the argument, without sanding down its voice.

A reasonable result would be an 8–15% reduction overall, driven by redundancy rather than a word-count quota. Volume 7 is likely to need the greatest compression.

## Phase 1 — Editorial control documents

Create four working documents before changing prose.

### 1. Argument map

For every chapter, record:

- Central thesis.
- Necessary premises.
- Dependency chapters.
- Novel contribution.
- Strongest objection.
- Function in the volume.
- Whether it should remain, merge, move, or be cut.

### 2. Canonical terminology sheet

Establish authoritative definitions and capitalization for the editors. This is the internal source of truth from which the reader-facing glossary in Phase 6 will be built.

Cover at least:

- Agency.
- Sovereignty.
- Measure, Credence, and Vantage.
- Viability.
- Coherence.
- Harm, coercion, consent, and force.
- Sentience and sapience.
- Constructor, pattern, schema, and egregore.
- Conditional objectivity and Sapient Agency Realism.

### 3. Claim-calibration standard

Classify consequential claims as one of:

- Established result.
- Interpretation of a result.
- Argument advanced by the book.
- Conjecture.
- Personal value commitment.
- Rhetorical illustration.

The prose should make these categories perceptible without becoming cluttered with labels.

### 4. Repetition ledger

Track recurring definitions, examples, slogans, and miniature summaries. Choose one canonical treatment for each and decide how much later chapters must repeat.

**Exit:** Every chapter has an entry in the argument map; the terminology sheet, calibration standard, and repetition ledger are ready to govern manuscript edits.

## Phase 2 — Complete architecture audit

Read only the Preface, Introduction, nine volume introductions, and final chapter of each volume.

Test:

- Does every volume have a distinct job?
- Does its conclusion deliver what its introduction promises?
- Is every dependency established before it is used?
- Does each volume materially advance the central agency argument?
- Are any volumes really two books joined together?
- Does the progression culminate naturally in Volume 9?

Produce a short structural memo for each volume before editing chapters. Do not rewrite volume introductions yet; they should describe the volumes that survive editing.

**Exit:** Nine structural memos and a confirmed or revised book-wide architecture.

## Phase 3 — Foundational volumes

### Volume 1 — The Physics of Agency and the Branching Universe

Progress: **Edited; awaiting author review.** See `book/editorial/volume-edits/01-physics-of-agency.md`.

This receives the first full edit because almost every later volume borrows its ontology.

- Separate standard physics from Axio's interpretations and original proposals.
- Audit the derivation and scope of the kybit and the three laws of agency.
- Clarify whether the Quantum Branching Universe is assumed, defended, or derived.
- Stress-test the transitions from Everettian physics to agency and free will.
- Examine the Modal Realization Principle, Chaos, coherence filters, and constructors for hidden assumptions.
- Present “why there is something” as a conditional dissolution rather than an unconditional metaphysical proof.
- Remove uses of physical vocabulary that are metaphorical but sound formally physical.

**Exit:** A skeptical technical reader can identify precisely where accepted physics ends and the book's philosophical construction begins.

### Volume 2 — Conditionalism: Truth, Bayes, and Rationality

- Make the self-application of “all truth is conditional” explicit and stable.
- Distinguish Conditionalism from contextualism, relativism, holism, and ordinary qualification.
- Tighten the three-level theory of truth.
- Remove repeated defenses against relativism once the canonical argument is established.
- Verify the relationship among Measure, Credence, Bayesian updating, and the Born rule.
- Ensure anthropic arguments state their sampling assumptions openly.
- Give “Probability After Probabilism” a clear role in the sequence.

**Exit:** Conditionalism can be summarized as a compact set of claims, and each later chapter demonstrably follows from or qualifies them.

### Volume 3 — Minds and Machines

- Consolidate the definitions of model, mind, consciousness, awareness, sentience, sapience, intelligence, and agency.
- Test whether the Modeler-Schema account explains consciousness or specifies its functional correlates.
- Separate phenomenological, functional, and moral claims.
- Reduce overlap among the AI-understanding and machine-intelligence chapters.
- Check empirical claims about IQ, cognition, LLMs, and AI behavior for durability and calibration.
- Remove topical AI commentary that will date quickly unless it illustrates a general mechanism.
- Strengthen the transition from consciousness theory to AI politics.

**Exit:** Every mental category has a stable definition, and no conclusion about machine moral status follows merely from terminological drift.

### Volume 4 — Axionic Agency

This is the formal center and should receive the most demanding evidentiary review.

- Distinguish theorem, experimental result, engineering demonstration, interpretation, and aspiration.
- Make every claimed invariant state its assumptions and jurisdiction.
- Clarify the relationship among the sovereign kernel, reflective stability, structural alignment, and verifiable agency.
- Connect program phases to papers and experiments without turning the volume into a laboratory diary.
- Explain negative results and remaining failure modes as prominently as successes.
- Audit claims such as “possibility became real” against what was actually demonstrated.
- Keep the final program retrospective concise and technically honest.

**Exit:** A reader can reconstruct what the research program established, what it merely supported, and what remains open.

### Volume 5 — Value and Agency-Centered Ethics

This is the normative hinge.

- Reconcile the opening rejection of objective value with the later introduction of Sapient Agency Realism.
- Make agent-binding operational rather than merely rhetorical.
- Stress-test value-as-sacrifice against addiction, compulsion, misinformation, and constrained choice.
- Ensure definitions of coercion, consent, property, and harm remain consistent in later volumes.
- Clarify the is–ought bridge from viability facts to chosen ethical commitments.
- Distinguish protection of agency from maximization of agency.
- Preserve the unresolved hard case while stating the contradiction and its consequences exactly.
- Check whether “risk is harm” over-expands the concept of harm.
- Prevent the coexistence protocol from quietly acquiring the centralized authority it rejects.

**Exit:** The ethical invariant follows transparently from stated commitments, with no hidden moral realism or utility aggregation.

## Phase 4 — Applied volumes

### Volume 6 — Markets, Money, and Prosperity

- Separate economic theory from empirical and historical claims.
- Verify or qualify claims about poverty, regulation, tariffs, monopoly, Bitcoin, nuclear energy, demographics, and intellectual property.
- Reduce repetition of subjective-value foundations already established in Volume 5.
- Identify boundary conditions under which market coordination fails.
- Ensure criticisms of intervention use the same causal standards as defenses of markets.
- Preserve the fictional coda if it illuminates the argument without introducing new doctrine.

**Exit:** The volume applies the prior value theory consistently and distinguishes mechanism, evidence, and advocacy.

### Volume 7 — Liberty, Coercion, and Governance

At roughly 95,000 words, this is the strongest candidate for substantial compression.

- Preserve the mature Grey Zone position and revise earlier categorical passages around it.
- Reconcile the earlier treatment of incitement as non-coercive speech with its later classification as delegated violence.
- Apply reciprocity and symmetry tests to the book's preferred institutions.
- Separate arguments against state monopoly from arguments that governance functions are unnecessary.
- Clarify the unresolved national-defense problem.
- Cut repeated critiques of socialism, communism, egalitarianism, and collective agency where they add no new mechanism.
- Ensure Axiocracy answers the enforcement, capture, succession, and error-correction problems raised earlier.
- Consider stronger internal part divisions even if the online volume remains formally unified.

**Exit:** The mature conditional position governs the whole volume, major objections remain visible, and repeated polemics have been consolidated.

### Volume 8 — Culture, Memetics, and Ideology

- Define what explanatory work “patterns as players” performs beyond metaphor.
- Apply the Axionic Criterion consistently to friendly and hostile ideologies.
- Separate diagnosis of a mechanism from judgment about a contemporary faction.
- Steelman every contested application before applying the framework.
- Check that examples remain intelligible after their immediate controversy fades.
- Reduce repeated descriptions of capture, bad faith, and classifier corruption.
- Retain earned polemic while removing passages that merely signal tribal alignment.

**Exit:** One symmetric analytical standard governs every case, and topical examples serve durable arguments.

### Volume 9 — Meaning, Spirituality, and the Secular Sacred

- Separate arguments the book claims to establish from the author's chosen credo.
- Avoid re-arguing the preceding book in compressed form.
- Test the claim that preserving one's origin is required for machine coherence rather than merely valuable.
- Keep the distinction between authentic transcendent experience and supernatural interpretation precise.
- Protect the emotional register of the double envoi from excessive explanatory scaffolding.
- Ensure the final letter welcomes believing readers without weakening the book's naturalism or sounding apologetic.

**Exit:** The conclusion follows from the book without pretending chosen meaning has been deductively established, and the double envoi retains its force.

## Phase 5 — Cross-volume reconciliation

Run dedicated continuity passes for:

- Agency and sovereignty.
- Measure, Credence, and branching.
- Consciousness, sentience, and sapience.
- Coherence and viability.
- Value and conditional objectivity.
- Harm, coercion, consent, and justified force.
- Patterns, selection, and cultural agency.
- Sacredness, meaning, and chosen commitments.

For each concept, identify:

- Its canonical defining chapter.
- Every later use.
- Any changed definition.
- Any contradiction.
- Whether later chapters should repeat, summarize, or cross-link.

Resolve the incitement, objective-value, moral-realism, and institutional-authority seams during this phase.

**Exit:** No material cross-volume contradiction remains unacknowledged, and every load-bearing term has one canonical treatment.

## Phase 6 — Reader-facing glossary

Build a published glossary only after cross-volume reconciliation has stabilized the terminology. The glossary is a reader aid, not a substitute for defining terms where the argument first introduces them.

- Create `book/00-front/03-glossary.md` and include it in the book's front matter and navigation.
- Derive every entry from the canonical terminology sheet; do not create competing definitions.
- Cover all terms of art whose meaning is specific to *Axio* or narrower than common usage.
- Give each entry a concise definition, the term's canonical defining chapter, and links to important later applications.
- Distinguish related terms explicitly, especially Measure/Credence, sentience/sapience, harm/coercion/force, agency/sovereignty, and coherence/viability.
- Mark terms that name conjectures, proposed frameworks, metrics, or personal value commitments so the glossary does not inflate their epistemic status.
- Add links to the glossary from the first natural use of specialized terms where this materially helps navigation.
- Verify that glossary wording matches every canonical use across the manuscript.

**Exit:** A reader can locate and distinguish every load-bearing term without encountering a definition that conflicts with the chapters.

## Phase 7 — Compression and prose

Perform three distinct passes.

### 1. Structural compression

Merge, move, or remove chapters that make the same argumentative move.

### 2. Chapter compression

Cut repeated premises, enumerations, throat-clearing, and conclusions that merely restate the chapter.

Review every chapter above the style guide's 3,000-word target, but retain longer chapters when their structure earns the length.

### 3. Line editing

Improve rhythm, clarity, transitions, pronoun consistency, examples, and sentence-level precision. Preserve the direct first-person voice. The goal is not to make *Axio* sound academic; it is to make its confidence exactly match its argument.

**Exit:** Every surviving chapter has a distinct function, no section exists solely through repetition, and the prose retains the author's voice.

## Phase 8 — Framing material

After the manuscript has stabilized, revise:

- Volume introductions.
- Preface.
- Book introduction.
- Chapter titles and subtitles.
- Cross-volume transitions.
- Volume conclusions.

The front matter should describe the book that survived editing, not the first draft's intentions.

**Exit:** The book's framing accurately promises the argument the final manuscript delivers.

## Phase 9 — Verification and promotion

Before changing any chapter to `final`:

- Run the full book build and link validator.
- Pin the Markdown/Pandoc toolchain for deterministic HTML.
- Check all source IDs and paper links.
- Search for obsolete terminology and superseded claims.
- Verify glossary anchors, defining-chapter links, and first-use links.
- Search for TODOs, dated news pegs, and blog furniture.
- Verify chapter ordering and navigation.
- Read each volume continuously rather than only chapter by chapter.
- Read Volumes 1–5 continuously as the book's load-bearing sequence.
- Read Volumes 6–9 for dependence on removed or revised material.

Promote status deliberately:

`draft → review` after structural and argumentative editing.

`review → final` after author approval, continuity review, copyedit, and successful build verification.

**Exit:** All chapters are final, the build is reproducible and clean, and the published book matches the reviewed source manuscript.

## Per-volume workflow

Apply the same workflow to each volume:

1. Write the structural memo.
2. Complete the chapter argument map.
3. Record author decisions on contested claims and proposed cuts.
4. Perform the structural edit.
5. Perform the argumentative and calibration edit.
6. Perform the terminology and continuity pass.
7. Perform compression and line editing.
8. Read the volume continuously.
9. Revise its introduction and conclusion.
10. Promote chapters from `draft` to `review`.

## Immediate next milestone

The first concrete deliverable is the book-wide argument map plus nine structural memos. This will reveal which changes are genuinely necessary before sentence-level attention is spent across 550,000 words.
