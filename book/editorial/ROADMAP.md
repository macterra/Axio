## Editorial goals

The edit should make *Axio*:

- More rigorous at the transitions between physics, philosophy, and ethics.
- Less repetitive when read sequentially without sacrificing volume independence.
- Clear about what is established, inferred, proposed, or speculative.
- Terminologically consistent across all nine volumes.
- Better calibrated in controversial and empirical passages.
- Shorter where compression strengthens the argument, without sanding down its voice.

A reasonable result would be an 8–15% reduction overall, driven by redundancy rather than a word-count quota. Volume 7 is likely to need the greatest compression.

## Phase 1 — Editorial control documents

Progress: **Complete.** The four control documents are established and were updated throughout the volume passes.

Create four working documents before changing prose.

### 1. Argument map

For every chapter, record:

- Central thesis.
- Necessary premises.
- Dependency chapters.
- Novel contribution.
- Strongest objection.
- Function in the volume.
- Whether it should remain, merge, move, or be cut.

### 2. Canonical terminology sheet

Establish authoritative definitions and capitalization for the editors. This is the internal source of truth from which the reader-facing glossary in Phase 6 will be built.

Cover at least:

- Agency.
- Sovereignty.
- Measure, Credence, and Vantage.
- Viability.
- Coherence.
- Harm, coercion, consent, and force.
- Sentience and sapience.
- Constructor, pattern, schema, and egregore.
- Conditional objectivity and Sapient Agency Realism.

### 3. Claim-calibration standard

Classify consequential claims as one of:

- Established result.
- Interpretation of a result.
- Argument advanced by the book.
- Conjecture.
- Personal value commitment.
- Rhetorical illustration.

The prose should make these categories perceptible without becoming cluttered with labels.

### 4. Repetition ledger

Track recurring definitions, examples, slogans, and miniature summaries. Choose one canonical treatment for each and decide how much later chapters must repeat.

**Exit:** Every chapter has an entry in the argument map; the terminology sheet, calibration standard, and repetition ledger are ready to govern manuscript edits.

Build a published glossary only after cross-volume reconciliation has stabilized the terminology. The glossary is a reader aid, not a substitute for defining terms where the argument first introduces them.

## Phase 7 — Compression and prose

Progress: **Pending.** Volume-level compression occurred during the merged passes; the dedicated continuous-reading and cross-volume compression pass remains.

Perform three distinct passes.

### 1. Structural compression

Merge, move, or remove chapters that make the same argumentative move.

### 2. Chapter compression

Cut repeated premises, enumerations, throat-clearing, and conclusions that merely restate the chapter.

Review every chapter above the style guide's 3,000-word target, but retain longer chapters when their structure earns the length.

### 3. Line editing

Improve rhythm, clarity, transitions, pronoun consistency, examples, and sentence-level precision. Preserve the direct first-person voice. The goal is not to make *Axio* sound academic; it is to make its confidence exactly match its argument.

**Exit:** Every surviving chapter has a distinct function, no section exists solely through repetition, and the prose retains the author's voice.

## Phase 9 — Verification and promotion

Progress: **Pending.** Full builds have passed during editing, but reproducibility, final link/source checks, author approval, and promotion to `final` remain.

Before changing any chapter to `final`:

- Run the full book build and link validator.
- Pin the Markdown/Pandoc toolchain for deterministic HTML.
- Check all source IDs and paper links.
- Search for obsolete terminology and superseded claims.
- Verify glossary anchors, defining-chapter links, and first-use links.
- Search for TODOs, dated news pegs, and blog furniture.
- Verify chapter ordering and navigation.
- Read each volume continuously rather than only chapter by chapter.
- Read Volumes 1–5 continuously as the book's load-bearing sequence.
- Read Volumes 6–9 for dependence on removed or revised material.

Promote status deliberately:

`draft → review` after structural and argumentative editing.

`review → final` after author approval, continuity review, copyedit, and successful build verification.

**Exit:** All chapters are final, the build is reproducible and clean, and the published book matches the reviewed source manuscript.

## Per-volume workflow

Apply the same workflow to each volume:

1. Write the structural memo.
2. Complete the chapter argument map.
3. Record author decisions on contested claims and proposed cuts.
4. Perform the structural edit.
5. Perform the argumentative and calibration edit.
6. Perform the terminology and continuity pass.
7. Perform compression and line editing.
8. Read the volume continuously.
9. Revise its introduction and conclusion.
10. Promote chapters from `draft` to `review`.
