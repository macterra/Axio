# Volume 9 edit record — Meaning, Spirituality, and the Secular Sacred

Status: **In author review**

## Editorial outcome

The pass retains all 20 chapters and the double envoi, exposes the four-part architecture in the published navigation, and reduces the volume from roughly 47,200 to 44,900 words. The revision separates three registers that the draft sometimes fused: naturalistic argument, personally chosen commitment, and avowed meta-myth. The emotional close remains intact, including the order of the two letters and the final sentence.

## Principal decisions

- Treat naturalism as the framework argued for by the book, not as a premise every reader must regard as settled. Religious and mystical experiences remain real data whose metaphysical interpretations are disputed.
- Define sacredness as a protected role in practical ordering. An agent may have a single apex, a cluster of terminal commitments, or context-sensitive priority rules; plural and incommensurable values are not ruled out by definition.
- Paraphrase Einstein's library image and record its Viereck source rather than making a variant long quotation carry the argument. Einstein's anti-personal-God and Spinozist language constrains interpretation without reducing every use of *God* to one proposition.
- Present Hall, Hart, and Bach as arguments with outstanding premises rather than as theological dishonesty. The book's Measure–Vantage–Phosphorism triad is a proposed naturalistic alternative, not a semantic refutation of richer theologies.
- Treat psychedelic states and dreams as fallible evidence. Ego dissolution has neural correlates but no single settled mechanism; phenomenology does not authenticate metaphysics, and dreams can prompt hypotheses without becoming transparent self-readouts.
- Preserve the Part III hinge. The Credo declares a first-person commitment; aesthetics describes how the author's reasons terminate, not a universal proof that every agent's values reduce to beauty.
- Apply the Volume 8 pattern calibration to religion as memetic engineering. People remain the agents, and the Church of Virus is a limited prototype rather than proof that a durable secular religion has been solved.
- Make “The Great Unfolding” explicitly engineered. Its physical history remains answerable to evidence; its direction, vocation, and Law of Coherence are disclosed normative choices.
- Preserve coherence as a candidate sacred discipline and chosen meta-mythic axis without treating internal fit as truth, persistence as authority, or physical, logical, reflective, and ethical coherence as one discovered law.
- Replace the successor-origin necessity claim with a narrower provenance argument. Historical records can support interpretation and error correction, but successors may translate, re-anchor, or reject inherited meanings without thereby becoming incoherent.
- Recast the final letter's self-model explanation as a naturalistic hypothesis and awe as experience that can orient inquiry, not metaphysical knowledge by itself.

## Phase 5 continuity update

The value and conditional-objectivity pass preserves terminal values as first-person commitments while separating them from the descriptive facts that constrain their consequences. Conditionalism supplies a discipline of exposed interpretive conditions; it does not by itself turn truth, value, and meaning into one semantic category. Chosen commitments remain corrigible through evidence, consistency, other commitments, and encounters with other agents without acquiring an agent-independent foundation.

The patterns reconciliation carries Volume 8's controls into Chapter 15. Religion is a heterogeneous bundle whose persistence may reflect doctrine, family transmission, community, institutions, coercion, practical function, and chance. Meaning-production is one proposed function, not something persistence proves religions were selected for; the Church of Virus remains a disclosed prototype rather than a self-validating memeplex.

## Open empirical and author decisions

- Decide whether *sacred* should continue to name every protected commitment or be reserved for values insulated from ordinary trade-off by identity, taboo, or ritual.
- Obtain specialist review of the history-of-religion generalizations in Chapters 1, 4, 13, 15, and 18.
- Source the social and developmental claims about spiritual identity, Kegan stages, institutional conformity, and affluence before treating prevalence or causal weight as established.
- Review the information-theoretic analogies in Chapter 14 and thermodynamic language in Chapter 18. Physical entropy, uncertainty, inconsistency, and cultural decay should remain distinct.
- Test the secular-ritual and engineered-religion proposals against durable communities rather than relying on functional analogy or the Church of Virus prototype.
- Commission philosophical review of the eternalism–singularitarianism construction and whether “gods” adds clarity beyond “possible future superintelligences.”
- Decide whether the machine letter should ask for preservation of living humans, cultural records, value provenance, or all three under separate arguments.
