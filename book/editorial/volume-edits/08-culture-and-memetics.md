# Volume 8 edit record — Culture, Memetics, and Ideology

Status: **In author review**

## Editorial outcome

The pass retains all 27 chapters, makes the five-part structure visible, and reduces the volume from roughly 67,400 to 63,700 words. The applied sequence now begins with a control case in which a historical claim survives scrutiny. Cultural selection, institutional capture, and asymmetry are presented as models and heuristics with explicit evidentiary limits rather than as literal group agency or motive-reading devices.

## Principal decisions

- Keep people as the choosing and responsible agents. Pattern language compresses causal loops of copying, reward, enforcement, and persistence; egregore and organism language is explicitly analogical.
- Treat bad-faith tactics as warning signs rather than direct access to intention. Repetition, response to correction, comparative standards, and independent evidence determine the diagnosis.
- Define institutional capture as a sustained, asymmetric failure of error correction. Compare resource scarcity, competence, coordination, law, professional quality control, and ordinary disagreement before inferring ideological protection.
- Reframe professional capture as a comparative institutional inquiry. Diagnostic revision is not capture by itself, universities were never outside politics, political speech does not forfeit protection from retaliation, and engineering combines universal physical constraints with situated objectives.
- Move “When the Claim Is True” to the start of Part V. The Franklin chapter rejects both the decisive-stolen-photograph myth and the dismissive supporting-technician myth; under-credit is well supported, while the causal share of sexism is stated more narrowly.
- Treat the two-ontology gender frame as a proposal that does not decide policy jurisdiction. Treat occupational disparity as underdetermined rather than as proof of either discrimination or unconstrained preference.
- Replace topical wealth and university-posting claims with durable examples or primary sources. The parity chapter now uses 2024 BLS fatal-injury data; the categorical-remedy case uses the official CIHR REDI eligibility rule.
- Replace the civilizational slavery ledger with local adjudication. Slavery's global history, the distinctive Atlantic system, European participation, and abolition can all be stated without converting history into inherited collective guilt or credit.
- Calibrate the Rotherham case to the Jay inquiry and the 2025 national audit. Ethnicity can be operationally relevant without establishing religious motive or licensing collective blame; resource and institutional explanations can coexist with a selective taboo.
- Make the asymmetry test a controlled-comparison heuristic. It can expose a missing distinction or falsify a simple explanation, but it does not identify a unique hidden term or motive without further evidence.
