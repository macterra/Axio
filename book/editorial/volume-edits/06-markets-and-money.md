# Volume 6 edit record — Markets, Money, and Prosperity

Status: **In author review**

## Editorial outcome

The pass preserves 26 Volume 6 chapters while replacing its borrowed political ending. “The Price of Breath” now follows Volume 7 as a draft coda, where its adjudication, liability, privacy, and Axiocracy institutions have actually been introduced. A new conclusion, “Coordination Is Not Salvation,” closes the economics argument and hands the unresolved question of legitimate coercion to governance.

The substantive edit applies a symmetric causal standard. A price is neither a moral oracle nor evidence of failure by itself. Property, liability, regulation, public provision, competition, and monetary policy are mechanisms whose boundary conditions and institutional costs must be stated. Neither *market* nor *state* functions as an automatic acquittal.

## Principal decisions

- Treat sacrifice, attention, observed exchange, price, and voluntary funding as defeasible evidence of value. Duress, addiction, misinformation, purchasing power, free-riding, transaction costs, and missing mechanisms can distort what is observed.
- Recast the capitalism trial as comparative institutional analysis. Unclear rights, weak liability, monetary distortion, and regulatory capture explain many failures but do not exhaust private power, information asymmetry, coordination failure, transaction costs, history, or public capacity.
- Correct the lighthouse case: privately operated British lights often relied on legally backed dues and grants. The history defeats categorical impossibility, not the public-goods mechanism.
- Distinguish inequality from poverty without declaring concentration neutral. Bargaining power, political influence, risk, positional goods, entry, and realistic exit connect distribution to agency.
- Preserve the case against broad tariffs while stating the assumptions and limits of comparative advantage, adjustment costs, security arguments, and China's mixed political economy.
- Subject antitrust and minimum-wage claims to evidence. State monopoly raises a reciprocity problem but does not make private exclusion consensual; minimum-wage effects are empirical and institution-dependent rather than arithmetic alone.
- Present MMT accurately as naming inflation and real resources as constraints. Hyperinflations are warnings about fiscal and institutional breakdown, not controlled refutations of a heterogeneous school.
- Narrow “cannot regulate math” to a claim about perfect semantic exclusion. Protocol rules can materially alter cost, prevalence, relay, and defaults while residual encoding channels remain.
- Compare proof-of-work and proof-of-stake through measured concentration and threat models. Proportional staking rewards do not mathematically force share concentration; energy, hardware, pools, custody, delegation, sanctions, and governance create different chokepoints.
- Mark Bitcoin's organism, autopoiesis, mind, and sovereignty language as metaphor. Institutional resilience does not satisfy the book's criteria for sapient sovereign agency.
- Treat prediction markets, insurance, MAID safeguards, and legalization constraints as mechanism proposals requiring benchmarks, consent, privacy, appeal, and evidence. Update the Canadian mental-illness eligibility date to March 17, 2027.
- Use child mortality and life expectancy as powerful but incomplete outcomes. The Rome codex is a dependency thought experiment, and the 2051 life-expectancy forecast is a dated personal Credence rather than a model output.
- Recast peak oil as a failed forecast on stated horizons, not proof that resource limits or catastrophe models generally fail. Adaptation is endogenous but neither free nor guaranteed.
- Present the nuclear chapter's $25 trillion total as an illustrative scenario under selected inputs, not a conservative estimate. A publishable estimate still needs a reproducible model, sources, uncertainty, and double-counting audit.
- Treat fertility and earnings as multicausal. Optionality and biological interruption remain hypotheses alongside housing, work, partnership, policy, discrimination, bargaining, selection, and measurement.
- Present weaker intellectual property as a policy argument, not a result guaranteed by physics. Open-source licensing itself uses copyright, and digital enforcement can change behavior without becoming perfect.
- Narrow AGI economics to its licensed conclusion: comparative advantage does not guarantee human wages or bargaining power. Near-zero replication cost, labor displacement, economic dependency, and existential risk remain separate conditional premises.

## Open empirical and author decisions

- **Macroeconomics:** decide whether the MMT chapter should remain a prosecution or become a comparison among MMT, Keynesian, monetarist, fiscal-theory, and Austrian mechanisms.
- **Poverty causation:** add comparative causal evidence separating trade, institutions, technology, public health, education, infrastructure, geography, and state capacity.
- **Market failures:** choose case studies with matched causal standards for successful and failed public and private remedies.
- **Bitcoin:** date and source energy, mining, pool, validator, relay, custody, and censorship measurements; define the threat model used to prefer proof-of-work.
- **Prediction markets:** publish the Calgary design, questions, participation, baseline forecasts, scoring rule, and outcomes before treating it as evidence of accuracy.
- **MAID:** test the incentive hypothesis against Canadian request, support-access, withdrawal, ineligibility, provider, and regional data; clinically and legally review the proposed safeguards.
- **Nuclear counterfactual:** publish a reproducible generation and health model or remove the aggregate dollar total. Treat displacement, costs, mortality, and overlapping ledgers probabilistically.
- **Fertility:** distinguish period from cohort fertility, desired from realized births, and timing from completed family size across countries and policy regimes.
- **Earnings:** source decomposition studies and state the population, outcome, controls, selection assumptions, and residual before assigning causal shares.
- **Intellectual property:** separate patents, copyright, trademarks, trade secrets, and sector-specific evidence; identify transition finance for research with high fixed costs.
- **AGI economics:** model compute, energy, capital, deployment, demand, ownership, and distribution before forecasting the disappearance of human complements.

## Repetition and voice

Chapters 1–5 own the economic layer of value, price, money, capital, and attention without reopening Volume 5's subjectivism. Chapter 6 owns the comparative failure taxonomy. Chapters 12–14 are a conditional Bitcoin module. Chapters 15–18 are the constructive mechanism-design center. Chapters 19–25 keep trends, mechanisms, scenario calculations, and forecasts distinct. The new coda owns the sentence the applied volumes need: coordination is a tool of agency, not a source of legitimate authority.

The pass preserves the volume's argumentative and first-person voice while removing false dichotomies, unsupported universals, stale current numbers, and rhetorical totals presented as measurements. Advocacy remains explicit where evidence does not select one institution uniquely.
