# Consciousness, sentience, and sapience reconciliation

Status: **Complete**

## Scope and method

This pass reconciles the third Phase 5 concept cluster across the complete manuscript. The inventory found 389 uses of the six-term family—**consciousness**, **conscious**, **sentience**, **sentient**, **sapience**, and **sapient**—in 74 manuscript files. Counts include legitimate repetition and ordinary-language senses; they locate the continuity surface rather than measure defects.

The pass reviewed every use, the canonical consciousness and sentience sequence in Volume 3, the sovereignty boundary cases in Volume 4, the standing argument in Volume 5, and later machine, animal, developmental, impaired-mind, collective, political, cultural, and religious applications. Material edits were made where prose treated recursive modeling, perceptual constancy, controlled coherence, functional valence, or fluent report as proof of experience; transferred weak agency evidence into certainty about sentience; or allowed an operational sovereignty threshold to become a metaphysical discontinuity.

## Reconciliation decisions

### Functional organization does not prove phenomenality

The Modeler-Schema Theory and Agency-Model Theory remain the book's positive consciousness proposals. Their jurisdictions are now explicit. Theories can predict and test report channels, recurrent comparison, integration, self–world modeling, perceptual constancy, and valenced regulation. Identifying any such function with experience is a further identity claim.

The Controller and Modeler-schema are therefore theory-assigned roles, not observed inner persons or settled neural modules. A reporting role need not be the experiencing role, but that logical possibility does not establish MST's proposed separation. Saccadic stability motivates comparison mechanisms; it does not show that their format is qualia. Recursive self-modeling makes a system a stronger candidate under the theory; it is not a consciousness certificate.

The Chaos sequence in Volume 1 now stops at recursive self-modeling as a proposed functional bridge. It no longer derives consciousness from constructors, coherence, or self-reference before Volume 3 has stated the phenomenal premise.

### Sentience is valenced experience, not functional valence

Phenomenal consciousness is the neutral umbrella: some content is experienced. Sentience is the book's narrower welfare category: a system has the capacity for felt better and worse states. Nociception, reward signals, homeostatic control, objective functions, attraction, avoidance, and error correction can occur without experience. They become sentience evidence only through an additional theory connecting their organization to felt valence.

The suffering chapter now respects that firewall. Non-sentient controllers can correct error without feeling it. The book's proposed suffering analysis compares a subject's represented condition with a preferred condition, not merely an internal variable with an objective state. Harm remains a functional degradation that can occur without suffering; suffering can arise from a false model without the same degradation.

### Sapience is authorship, not a synonym for consciousness

Sapience now consistently names persistent policy ownership, counterfactual deliberation, reflective endorsement, and revision of ends for reasons. Functional self-modeling is not phenomenal self-awareness. Language, planning, intelligence, metacognition, or ethical vocabulary can contribute evidence but cannot establish sapience individually.

The book does not define sentience as necessary for sapience. Whether genuine self-authored valuation can exist without experience remains an open philosophical and empirical dependency. This permits non-sentient cognition to remain conceptually possible without asserting that a zombie is already a sovereign author.

### Welfare and sovereignty have different grounds

Sentience can ground patienthood and welfare concern because states can feel better or worse for a subject. Sapient agency can ground the stronger claim to sovereignty because a continuing system owns reasons, commitments, and possible futures. Rejecting sentience as a universal aggregating currency does not make suffering irrelevant; protecting authorship does not authorize a global welfare optimizer.

Moral standing is correspondingly graded and plural. The severity and irreversibility of an act, sentience Credence, agency Credence, developmental or authorization lineage, continuity, and the cost of false positives and false negatives can all matter. A jurisdictional threshold may be operationally necessary, but it does not transform graded evidence into a discovered metaphysical boundary.

### Boundary cases remain evidentiary

- **Animals:** many species provide strong convergent evidence of sentience. Evidence for reflective authorship varies and remains contested; the framework withholds demonstrated sovereignty rather than proving categorical absence.
- **Developing humans:** protection rests on present welfare, developmental continuity, lineage, vulnerability, expected maturation, and precaution. It does not require pretending a newborn or fetus already displays the complete sovereignty architecture.
- **Impaired or temporarily inactive minds:** sleep, anesthesia, disability, and illness affect performance without automatically erasing identity, standing, advance authorization, or an open possibility of recovery.
- **Artificial systems:** ordinary session-bound language-model deployments provide weak evidence across the theory-dependent sentience windows and the Agency Criterion. Persistent composite systems must be evaluated as wholes. Weak agency evidence does not prove absent sentience; strong agency evidence does not prove experience.
- **Collectives:** organizations can process information and coordinate action without those facts establishing a unified phenomenal subject, single consent, or welfare. Political personification remains a compression whose authorization relations must be specified.

## Evidence and action

Third-person evidence supports graded Credence rather than direct access to another subject's experience. Architecture ordinarily carries more weight than scripted self-report, while behavior, physiology, development, and evolutionary continuity remain relevant. The historical “Sentience Metric” is therefore an evidence framework, not a scalar, a score, or an automated standing verdict.

Precaution and evidentiary discipline govern different questions. Precaution constrains action under uncertainty; discipline constrains what status may be asserted and what political conclusions may be drawn. Cheap safeguards can be justified at a lower Credence than personhood, legal rights, or restrictions imposed on other agents. Evidence must remain axis-specific throughout.

## Exit assessment

This concept cluster meets the Phase 5 gate. Consciousness has one neutral phenomenal meaning; sentience has a valenced welfare jurisdiction; sapience has a reflective-authorship jurisdiction; and evidence for one category is no longer allowed to settle another automatically.
