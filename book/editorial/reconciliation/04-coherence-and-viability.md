# Coherence and viability reconciliation

Status: **Complete**

## Scope and method

This pass reconciles the fourth Phase 5 concept cluster across the complete manuscript. The inventory found 930 uses of the eight-term family—**coherence**, **coherent**, **incoherence**, **incoherent**, **viability**, **viable**, **nonviable**, and **non-viable**—in 171 manuscript files. Counts include legitimate repetition, ordinary-language uses, and overlapping forms; they map the continuity surface rather than measure defects.

The inventory mapped every use, and close review concentrated on the canonical Chaos/coherence sequence, Conditionalism's truth and Sacred Coherence chapters, the Reflective Coherence Thesis, the viability sequence in Volume 5, and later load-bearing applications. Edits were required where prose turned a model-relative identity criterion into a presemantic fact, treated a formal filter as an autonomous physical selector, promoted internal fit into truth, predicted reflective convergence without an architecture or environment, or derived ethical authority from persistence.

## Reconciliation decisions

### Coherence is indexed rather than presemantic

Volume 1 retains identity preservation across transformation as the root *shape* of dynamical coherence. The claim now requires a grain of description, an identity criterion, a transformation class, and relevant constraints. Physical dynamics do not depend on being observed, but a statement that two states instantiate the same pattern still uses a model of sameness. An arbitrary mapping cannot establish identity merely by being non-degenerate.

The Chaos construction preserves its conjectural role. A formal exclusion predicate selects a represented subset; a semantic map creates equivalence classes under an interpretation; a physical process may favor stable structures. Moving among those registers requires a realization relation the current construction does not provide. Physics is therefore not presented as a catalogue derived from whatever “survived Chaos.”

### Epistemic coherence is necessary but not sufficient

Logical consistency and mutual fit keep claims discriminating and corrigible under declared inferential rules. They do not establish correspondence. Observation, intervention, prediction, replication, and target resistance remain independent constraints; a coherent fiction can still be false.

Sacred Coherence now consistently names a personal epistemic commitment rather than a universal theorem. The chapter no longer treats every rival ethical or religious system as formally incoherent, Gödel as a proof of the hierarchy, or physical and engineering success as direct confirmation of one logic. Coherence disciplines substantive values without supplying compassion, justice, or goodness from non-contradiction.

### Reflective coherence is conditional on architecture

The Reflective Coherence Thesis is restricted to agents whose goal semantics remain open to epistemically constrained self-interpretation. Any convergence claim must state an architecture, environment, revision rule, and stability criterion. Compartmentalization, external support, lock-in, and power can preserve destructive or inconsistent systems.

Semantic revision may be required under ontological refinement, but drift is not automatically stability and fixed components are not automatically pathological. The architecture must distinguish warranted reinterpretation from corruption, succession, or replacement and make cross-domain correction causally binding. Reflective coherence remains compatible with coherent monstrosity.

### Viability has declared indices

Every material viability claim must identify or make inferable:

- the unit being assessed;
- the identity, organization, or function to be preserved;
- the environment, constraints, and perturbations;
- the baseline for failure; and
- the time horizon.

An organism, reflective agent, preference architecture, institution, culture, and lineage can be viable in different senses. Persistence of one can consume the health or agency of another. Fossilization, replication, functional continuation, and successor continuity are therefore not interchangeable verdicts.

### Selection is not a universal scoreboard

The “ultimate metagame” remains an analytic lens for survivorship, not a literal game played by every pattern. Selection models require units, mechanisms, environments, and comparison classes. “Winning” means only persistence or propagation under that description; it does not imply truth, function, welfare, or value. Agents can exit games, end projects, transfer commitments, or sacrifice persistence for another end.

The QBU is optional and adds no normative force. Measure may weight specified modeled continuations, but it supplies neither the identity criterion nor a reason to value persistence.

### Ethical protection requires an avowed bridge

Descriptive viability facts can give hypothetical reasons to an agent that wants its agency, commitments, or successors to continue. They issue no unconditional instruction. Coercive and deceptive orders can persist; reciprocal respect is not the only equilibrium.

The Ethics of Viability therefore begins with a chosen commitment to protect reciprocal sapient authorship. Rights, standing, defensive permission, and procedure depend on that normative premise plus evidence, attribution, necessity, proportionality, due process, and institutional legitimacy. Predation can justify defense without automatically erasing all standing. The framework rejects domination because it violates its commitment, not because the universe guarantees domination will collapse.

## Exit assessment

This concept cluster meets the Phase 5 gate. Coherence has distinct logical, epistemic, dynamical, reflective, and personal-normative jurisdictions. Viability is indexed to a unit, function or identity, conditions, baseline, and horizon. Persistence and selection remain descriptive; ethical protection and sacred orientation disclose their chosen premises. The remaining questions about physical realization from Chaos, general convergence under reflection, cultural selection mechanisms, and institutional boundaries are acknowledged research or later-cluster questions rather than hidden derivations.

The next Phase 5 cluster is **value and conditional objectivity**.
