# Value and conditional objectivity reconciliation

Status: **Complete**

## Scope and method

This pass reconciles the fifth Phase 5 concept cluster across the complete manuscript. The broad inventory found 1,910 uses of the value/objectivity family in 215 manuscript files. Most are legitimate ordinary, economic, aesthetic, or epistemic uses. Close review concentrated on Volume 5's opening sequence, its later structural turn, the closing Sapient Agency Realism chapter, market-value applications in Volume 6, governance by value discovery in Volume 7, and chosen-value applications in Volume 9.

The principal defect was not simple terminological variation. Several passages moved too quickly among evidence about what an agent values, entailment from an evaluative standard, and authority over an agent who rejects that standard. Others allowed coherence, viability, suffering, or agency structure to supply a normative premise after the volume had denied that description could do so.

## Canonical hierarchy

| Layer | Canonical meaning | Canonical home | Does not imply |
|---|---|---|---|
| Descriptive fact | A claim about an agent, act, consequence, causal relation, or institutional condition, assessed under ordinary epistemic uncertainty. | Local empirical chapters and act models | A value, duty, permission, or standing by itself |
| Value | An agent-relative ordering of possibilities. Choice, tradeoff, and sacrifice provide defeasible evidence under stated conditions. | Vol. 5, chs. 1 and 4 | Whim, incorrigibility, or a preference conclusively revealed by behavior |
| Attribution | The empirical claim that an agent actually holds, endorses, or authorizes a commitment. | Vol. 5, ch. 2 | Sincerity, stability, coherence, complete articulation, or moral authority |
| Normative premise | The evaluative standard that supplies the ought in a bound claim. It may be personal, shared, or institutional, but its source and claimed jurisdiction must be stated. | Vol. 5, chs. 2, 6, 22, and 26 | Truth from nowhere or adoption by every person to whom a framework applies it |
| Conditional application | A verdict derived under a normative premise plus a factual model, agent or class, scope, baseline, evidence, horizon, and priority or conflict rules. | Vol. 5, chs. 2 and 26 | An empirical proposition merely because evidence enters its application |
| Conditional objectivity | The stipulated public assessability and repeatability of a conditional application under fixed inputs. | Vol. 5, chs. 2 and 26 | Agent-independent value, guaranteed agreement, motivation, authority, or legitimate enforcement |
| Sapient Agency Realism | An explicit reciprocal-authorship premise joined to facts about sapient agents and the framework's application rules. | Vol. 5, ch. 26 | An ought derived from agency structure; *conditional constructivism* remains an acknowledged alternative label |
| Institutional authority | A procedurally and jurisdictionally justified power to make or enforce public rules. | Vols. 5 and 7 | Legitimacy merely because a moral classification or conditional application is available |

## Reconciliation decisions

### Agent-relative does not mean arbitrary

The manuscript retains the Volume 5 axiom that value requires a valuer. The opening chapter now presents that position as a substantive relational thesis rather than a victory by definition or a refutation of every view called moral realism. Agent-relative values may be reflective, stable, shared, corrigible, and constrained in their application by evidence and causal facts. None of those properties makes a terminal normative premise agent-independent.

Observed sacrifice remains defeasible evidence rather than a transparent meter. Duress, compulsion, misinformation, poverty, adaptive preference, and constrained alternatives can separate behavior from reflective endorsement. Volume 6's market usage is already appropriately narrower: price and exchange reveal willingness and ability under specified institutions and purchasing power, not moral worth.

### Attribution and application are separate

Agent-binding now names a complete family of inputs rather than merely attaching a person's name to a verdict. It specifies an agent or class, normative standard, factual model, scope, baseline, evidence, horizon, and priority or conflict rules.

Two questions must remain distinct:

1. **Attribution:** does the agent actually hold or endorse the stated commitment?
2. **Application:** if the commitment and remaining inputs are fixed, does the verdict follow?

An action can conflict with an attributed commitment even if the attribution is later falsified. An agent can sincerely endorse a rule whose application remains indeterminate because consequences or conflict rules are unsettled. Binding repairs under-specification; it proves neither endorsement nor authority.

### Conditional objectivity is procedural and limited

The phrase now means public assessability and repeatability under fixed inputs. A normative application can be correct under an explicit premise without becoming an empirical claim. The premise remains open to endorsement, rejection, comparison, and revision.

The pass therefore separates six questions that earlier prose sometimes fused: premise selection, attribution, application, motivation, authority, and enforcement. Accuracy at one layer does not settle the next. In particular, a framework may apply its rule to conduct by a non-adopter without the target's rejection invalidating the application. That universal coverage within a declared domain is not universal adoption and does not prove agent-independent authority.

### Structure constrains application but supplies no terminal premise

Causal facts about suffering, coercion, deception, persistence, and agency loss can be robust and decision-relevant. They become welfare or sovereignty wrongs only under a premise that gives those interests standing. Coherence can expose self-defeat relative to an agent's own commitments; it does not guarantee that a coherent predator will share the framework's commitments. Viability can describe persistence; it does not choose what deserves protection.

The Ethics of Viability and Sapient Agency Realism therefore state the bridge openly: **given the commitment that each sovereign agent's authorship has standing**, specified evidence and application rules support verdicts about preservation and violation. The structure disciplines the application. It does not legislate the premise.

### Moral classification is not enforcement authority

A framework's ability to classify a defector, torturer, or institution does not by itself justify constraint, repair, punishment, or exclusion. Defensive permission and public enforcement additionally require causation, evidence, necessity, proportionality, due process, jurisdiction, and institutional legitimacy. This firewall carries directly into the next Phase 5 cluster on harm, coercion, consent, and justified force.

### Later applications preserve their local senses

Volume 6 distinguishes exchange value, price, and moral worth. Volume 7's Axiocracy treats value discovery as a conditional market signal weighted by purchasing power and bounded by political legitimacy; it is not rule by objective worth. Volume 9 preserves terminal valuing and chosen meaning while making clear that consequences remain answerable to evidence, other commitments, and other agents.

## Files with material continuity edits

Material manuscript changes are concentrated in:

- Volume 5: the volume introduction and Chapters 1–3, 5–7, 14, 16, 21, 26, and 27.
- Volume 9: Chapters 11 and 12.

The terminology authority, repetition ledger, roadmap, and relevant volume edit records now encode the same distinctions.

## Exit assessment

This concept cluster meets the Phase 5 gate. The manuscript consistently separates valuer-relative ordering, evidence of endorsement, normative premise, conditional application, public assessability, motivation, authority, and enforcement. Sapient Agency Realism no longer appears to recover the agent-independent value rejected at the opening: it is a stipulated framework with an avowed reciprocal-authorship premise and publicly criticizable applications.

The next Phase 5 cluster is **harm, coercion, consent, and justified force**.
