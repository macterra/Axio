**Thesis.** The counterweight chapter: a discrimination claim adjudicated as true, by the same standards the previous chapter used to reject others. Franklin was genuinely treated unfairly — not by literal theft but by a permissive culture in which individual ambition fit seamlessly into institutional sexism: Photo 51 shown by Wilkins without her consent, the MRC report routed to Crick, the deliberately vague Nature acknowledgment that "gestured at propriety while concealing dependence." The counterarguments are granted in full (the seminar disclosure, the technically-open report, same-issue publication) and shown to soften but not erase the case; the verdict is calibrated, not maximalist — the injustice lives in the fit between ambition and institutional bias, not in a single villainous act. The chapter's real subject is method: case-by-case adjudication, keeping light and shadow interlaced — "history does not owe forgiveness, only clarity."
