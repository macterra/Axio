**Thesis.** Capture begins with vocabulary — seizing labels, the past, and the future as rhetorical territory. The "liberal" label was hijacked: a philosophy of individual autonomy, property, and rule of law now names its statist opposite, with three consequences (philosophical obscurity, historical misappropriation, and the strategic prize of branding authentic liberals "right-wing"). The future gets captured too: "the right side of history" appeals to a nonexistent tribunal — conscripting an imagined future consensus to bypass argument, resting on four faulty presuppositions, and refuted by history's own catalogue of confidently held wrongs (eugenics, Prohibition, segregation); the phrase's real value is as a warning signal that inevitability has been substituted for argument. And numbers get captured: the "Musk owns more than the bottom half" comparison is a statistical artifact — the bottom 20% has negative aggregate net worth, so anyone solvent "outperforms" it — and the consistency test kills the metric (applied to its own promoters, it indicts them).
