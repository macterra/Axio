**Thesis.** The enforcement machinery, in escalating stages. Cancel culture is not free association gone large — it is the pathological escalation from choosing to disassociate to coercively enforcing disassociation on others: Tier 1, pure disassociation (fully legitimate — firing, boycotting, cutting ties); Tier 2, coordinated disassociation (the borderline between persuasion and mob coercion); Tier 3, suppression by proxy (censorship by proxy — smearing, harassing, targeting bystanders, organized platform-ban campaigns). The harm is never that people walk away; it is that they prevent anyone else from listening or affiliating. The terminal stage is the memory hole: worse than censorship because it rewrites the past to create the illusion the inconvenient truth never existed — "Forgetting is natural. Suppression is political. But memory-holing is totalitarian," because it annihilates the very possibility of remembering.
