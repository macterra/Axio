**Thesis.** David Deutsch's "Pattern" — the persistence of antisemitism across cultures and centuries — is best explained as a perverse Schelling point: a self-reinforcing coordination attractor whose stated justifications are all post-hoc. The rationalizations contradict one another (too powerful and too weak, too separate and too assimilated), which proves the logic is not primary; coordination is. Each activation reinforces the focal point, so robustness grows with use, and the attractor activates automatically under societal stress — the Black Death, Weimar, modern conspiracy culture. The uncomfortable consequence: debunking and education cannot dismantle it, because refuting the justifications leaves the coordination mechanism intact; displacement — alternative Schelling points, norms, narratives — is the only lever that touches the mechanism itself.
