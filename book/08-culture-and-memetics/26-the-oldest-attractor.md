David Deutsch calls it the Pattern: the recurring readiness of societies to license harm against Jews, appearing and reappearing across cultures that shared no border, no language, no god, and no memory of one another. It surfaces in Alexandria and in medieval Rhineland, in Tsarist Russia and in Weimar Germany, in societies with large Jewish populations and in societies with almost none. Pressed to give the reason for it, Deutsch declined — not out of delicacy but out of precision. He argued that the reasons are the wrong place to look, because every reason ever offered has been a rationalization assembled after the hostility, not a cause that produced it. That refusal is the beginning of an explanation, and it is the right one. What follows is an attempt to name the mechanism it points at.

I want to concede the full weight of the thing before analyzing it, because analysis of this particular subject curdles into evasion the moment it gets clever. The Pattern is not a metaphor and not a rhetorical figure. It has a body count that runs from pogrom to expulsion to industrialized murder, and it is not a historical curiosity: it is live, it is present, and it kills people now. Nothing in a structural account lightens any of that. The point of the account is the opposite. A phenomenon this durable and this lethal deserves to be understood at the level where it actually operates, and moralizing about it — however sincere — has demonstrably failed to end it. If the standard tools have not worked across two thousand years, the responsible move is to ask what those tools have been missing.

## Reasons That Cancel Each Other Out

Start with the tell. The charges laid against Jews do not merely vary across time and place; they directly contradict one another, and they contradict one another in a way that no coherent grievance could. Jews are indicted as rootless cosmopolitans with no loyalty to any nation and, in the same era, as a nation apart too clannish to assimilate. They are cast as the architects of rapacious capitalism and, simultaneously, as the authors of world communism. They are too rich and too parasitic; too powerful, secretly running the banks and the press and the governments, and too weak, a fit target precisely because they are defenseless. Too assimilated, hiding among us; too insular, refusing to join us. Every axis of accusation runs both directions at once.

When the reasons offered for a belief are mutually exclusive, the reasons are not what is holding the belief up. A person who hates Jews for being communists and also for being bankers is not reasoning from communism or from banking. The content of the charge is interchangeable; what persists is the target. This is the observation Deutsch refused to explain away, and it forecloses the entire genre of explanation that treats antisemitism as a mistaken inference — as if the haters had simply gotten some facts wrong and could be corrected with better ones. You cannot correct a conclusion that generates its own premises on demand. The premises are downstream. Something else is selecting the target, and the reasons are being manufactured afterward to fit.

That "something else" is the subject of this chapter, and it is not psychological in the first instance. It is a coordination structure.

## The Third Attractor, at Full Scale

This volume has used the word attractor at three scales, and warned that they are three views of one phenomenon rather than a shared metaphor. At the scale of individual conduct, an exemplar pulls imitation toward itself. At the scale of belief systems, an ideology curves the [cultural fitness landscape](06-the-geometry-of-culture.md) around itself until escape becomes nearly impossible — the black-hole attractor. The third kind, flagged in [the note on attractors](03-patterns-as-players.md), operates at the scale of whole civilizations: a pattern that recurrently re-forms across centuries because it solves a coordination problem no one will admit to having. The Pattern is that third attractor observed at its full historical extent, and it is worth being exact about the coordination problem it solves.

A Schelling point, in Thomas Schelling's original sense, is a solution that two parties converge on without communicating, chosen not because it is optimal but because it is obvious — because each party expects the other to expect it. Strangers told to meet in New York on a given day, with no time or place agreed, disproportionately choose noon at Grand Central, not because noon at Grand Central is correct but because each guesses the other will guess it. The focal point wins on salience, precedent, and the shared expectation of shared expectation. Its authority is entirely self-referential: it is the answer because everyone treats it as the answer.

Now transpose this into the domain of collective hostility. A society under strain reaches, often below the level of deliberate choice, for a target — someone to blame, to expel, to unite against. This is a coordination problem in the exact technical sense: the crowd needs to converge on the same target, and it needs to converge without anyone openly proposing the coordination, because openly proposing it would expose it as arbitrary. What the crowd needs is a focal point. And once a group has been made the target even once — for reasons that may have been entirely local and contingent — it becomes the salient answer to the question the next crisis asks. Precedent is the whole of the mechanism. The Jews are available as the target of a European pogrom in 1349 in part because they were the target of one before, which was itself legible as a target because of one before that. Each activation deepens the groove. The Pattern is not remembered as a policy; it is inherited as a reflex, a default that each generation finds already installed and each crisis finds ready to hand.

This is why the contradictory rationalizations are not a weakness in the phenomenon but a signature of it. A genuine grievance would be pinned to its content and would dissolve when the content was refuted. A Schelling point is pinned to nothing but its own salience, so it survives the refutation of any particular justification indefinitely — it simply issues a new one. The reasons are load-shifting rationalizations because the load was never on the reasons. It was on the focal point, and the focal point is held in place by the shared expectation that it will be chosen, which every prior choosing reinforces.

## Why It Fires Under Stress

Note when the attractor activates. Not at random, and not in proportion to anything Jews do. It fires when a society is under load — plague, defeat, inflation, humiliation, the sense that some vast and unnamed thing has gone wrong and someone must have done it. The Black Death, which no one in the fourteenth century could explain, produced the well-poisoning libel and the massacre of Rhineland Jewish communities that had nothing to do with the disease. Weimar Germany, staggering under defeat and hyperinflation, reached for the stab-in-the-back and found the focal point waiting. Modern conspiracy culture, confronting a world too complex and too impersonal to hold in the mind, reconstructs the same figure — the hidden hand, the string-puller, the cabal — and lands, with dispiriting reliability, on the oldest available name for it.

The regularity is the point. Under stress a population needs an explanation that is emotionally adequate to its distress, which means an explanation with an agent in it, a will behind the misfortune. Impersonal causes — a bacterium, a business cycle, the structural churn of a globalizing economy — do not satisfy the demand, because you cannot punish a bacterium and you cannot unite against a business cycle. The Pattern supplies an agent, and it supplies the same agent every time, because the same agent has been supplied every time before. The attractor is not created by each crisis. It is triggered by each crisis, the way a well-worn path is not laid down but merely followed. This is what it means for a coordination structure to be robust: robustness grows with use, and two thousand years of use have made this the most robust such structure in the human record. It is, in the most literal sense the title can bear, the oldest attractor we have.

## What Debunking Cannot Reach

Here the account turns from diagnosis to the uncomfortable consequence, and I will not soften it. If the Pattern is held in place by coordination rather than by belief, then the interventions aimed at belief cannot dismantle it. Refute the blood libel and the coordination survives, because the coordination was never resting on the blood libel; it was resting on the focal point, which now reaches for the next charge. Teach the history, publish the documentation, win every argument on the merits — and you will have corrected a great many individual errors while leaving the mechanism that generates them untouched. This is not an argument against education. Education is worth doing on its own terms, and refuting a specific lie protects the specific people that specific lie would have harmed. It is an argument against expecting education to reach the root. You are debunking the exhaust and calling it the engine.

The mechanism lives one level down, in the structure of expectation itself — in the fact that a strained crowd expects the crowd to converge here, and converges here because it expects to. To touch that, you have to touch the focal point, not the justifications hung on it. In principle this means displacement: supplying a rival focal point salient enough to catch the coordinating impulse before it reaches the old groove. A society that has a well-established, high-salience way of routing its crisis-blame elsewhere — toward the actual corruption, the actual mismanagement, the actual and nameable authors of its distress — is a society in which the old attractor has competition for the same coordinating energy.

I state the displacement thesis with less confidence than the diagnostic one, and the register should stay sober, because the honest version of this claim is narrow. It does not license the fantasy that the right public-service campaign dissolves a two-thousand-year attractor, and it does not treat "promote better narratives" as a solved problem — narratives are patterns competing in an ecology, and the destructive ones are often the fitter competitors precisely because they are simpler and more satisfying. What the framing yields is not a program but a correctness condition: any intervention that leaves the coordination structure intact is aimed at the wrong target, however true its content and however good its intentions. That is a real constraint. It rules out most of what is conventionally attempted, and it tells you the difference between fighting the symptom and reaching the mechanism, even when reaching the mechanism is hard.

## Two Failures of the Same Infrastructure

There is a symmetry here worth naming, because it binds this case back to the account of capture in the preceding part. [The forbidden pattern](21-the-forbidden-pattern.md) is the story of an institution taught that a real and dangerous pattern is unsafe to perceive, and so perceiving around it — a scotoma painted over so smoothly that no one inside feels blind. The Pattern is the mirror image. There, attention was trained to suppress a pattern that was actually present; here, attention is trained to manufacture a pattern that is not. One is a false negative in a society's perceptual system, the other a false positive, and both are failures of the same infrastructure: the moral machinery that decides what a collective is allowed to see and obliged to blame. The grooming-gang scotoma erases a guilty network that is really there. The antisemitic attractor conjures a guilty network that is not. In each case the error is upstream of evidence — it is in what the society has coordinated to look at, and to look away from — which is why in each case the flood of evidence, in whichever direction, never resolves it. A civilization that cannot govern its own attention will keep committing both errors, and it will keep experiencing each of them, from the inside, as simply seeing what is there.
