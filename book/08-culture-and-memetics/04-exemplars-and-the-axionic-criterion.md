**Thesis.** Agency develops through imitation before it ever runs on rules: children learn to stand by watching, and adult competence still rides on absorbed patterns ("posture, timing, pressure, restraint"). Exemplars are agents whose behavior functions as an attractor in behavioral space — influence scaling with the coherence, generality, and compressibility of the pattern — and cultural continuity is distributed instantiation: each agent realizing the pattern under local constraints. Religious figures are history's highest-bandwidth exemplars ("Jesus is Lord" read as a diagnostic of pattern centrality, not a metaphysical claim), and pattern survival works like a river: flowing, branching, eroding obstacles, retaining a recognizable course. The normative payoff is the Axionic Criterion, the volume's test for which patterns deserve propagation: does it support coherent choice under constraint, reduce reliance on coercive stabilization, and scale across agents without collapsing autonomy?
