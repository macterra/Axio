**Thesis.** Trauma narratives occupy an inseparable double register — healing (restoring narrative coherence to the sufferer) and power (tilting the social narrative terrain) — demanding discernment rather than automatic indulgence or suspicion: "is this story being told to stitch a soul, or to seize the stage? Often it is both." Three registers of the same currency. Psychological: the double register itself, steelmanning the cynical reading and then correcting it. Sociological: following Sowell, the decisive factor in group outcomes is the internal cultural response to adversity, not the severity of discrimination — and tying rewards to perceived oppression rather than achievement builds perverse incentives that perpetuate the inequality they decry; the remedy is structural universalism. Gendered: the infantilization reflex — full agency granted to women except where mate choices violate egalitarian expectations, at which point agency is rhetorically revoked ("power imbalance," "grooming-adjacent") — a politically useful incoherence: "either we accept adult agency in full, or we are engaged in paternalism wrapped in moral rhetoric."
