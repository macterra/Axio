**Thesis.** The gender debate is unresolvable as currently conducted because both camps use "performance" with inverted metaphysics: performance-as-imitation (Dawkins/Joyce — a falsehood enacted) versus performance-as-creation (Butler — a category constituted by enactment). The same sentence, "gender is performative," means opposite things: descriptive categories answer to correspondence (words must match biology); constitutive categories answer to enactment (words create social reality). Both sides are right within their own ontology, each sees in the other the sin of substituting ideology for reality (the mirror illusion), and the war is over the ontology of words — not morality, not science. The corrective that dissolves the stalemate: sex is empirically real; gender is socially real — different ontological layers with different truth conditions.
