**Thesis.** "Culture" splits into two distinct objects: Cultural Schemas (sets of beliefs) and Cultural Groups (the agents whose belief systems contain them) — with an elegant inverse relation: the larger and more restrictive the schema, the smaller the group. The formalism generates hierarchies naturally: the intersection of sibling schemas defines a parent schema (Christian ⊂ Catholic, Protestant), subset relations invert between schema-space and group-space, and the structure explains cultural transmission, divergence, convergence — and why shared higher-level schemas are the levers of reconciliation in inter-group conflict.
