**Thesis.** The gender equality paradox is symmetrical, and the symmetry is the tell. Freedom amplifies rather than erases sex-typical sorting (the Nordic paradox: fewer barriers, lower female STEM share; people-oriented vs thing-oriented vocational interests; male variance at both extremes) — and the same three explanations apply to the fields nobody marches about: over 90% of workplace deaths are male, from the same biology, interests, and freedom effect. Parity is demanded only in prestigious fields, never in deadly ones — "we do not march for equality in the morgue" — and the asymmetry reveals that the operative concern was never equality as such. The supply-chain accounting deepens it: across the full lifecycle of technology (extraction, heavy industry, logistics, installation), labor-hours run 80–95% male, with female-dominated fields (nursing, education) explained the same voluntary-sorting way. Real frictions for minority-sex entrants exist and merit voluntary remedies (mentorship, unbiased assessment, role models) — friction is not injustice, and the closing dilemma stands: accept unequal outcomes, or accept coercion.
