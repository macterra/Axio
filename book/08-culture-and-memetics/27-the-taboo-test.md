**Thesis.** The volume closer: one small case that carries the whole method. The European disdain for air conditioning is climate nostalgia dressed as environmental virtue — a genealogically explicable norm (winter was the old enemy, so heating became invisible and dignified while cooling arrived coded American and decadent) that is now lethal, since heat kills Europeans in numbers ("that should be the end of the etiquette"). The energy ledger runs the wrong way (heating dwarfs cooling in European household energy — if energy were the worry, winter would be the target), the objections dissolve in turn (urban heat is mostly albedo and traffic; dirty peakers indict grid planning; old buildings are a design requirement, not a moral argument), and the heat-pump test seals it: the same machine, virtuous in January and suspect in July — "the verdict was never about energy. It was about symbolism." Generalized, this is the volume's reusable instrument: find the asymmetry, name the real variable. Heating/cooling; prestige/peril; authorized/unauthorized discrimination; the trending topics a gated platform cannot see — every standing position in this book was audited with the same tool.
