**Thesis.** The Part IV capstone and the volume's canonical capture theory. The British grooming-gang scandal was an institutional attention failure, not an information failure: moralized attention taught institutions that one category of fact was unsafe to perceive. The order of guilt is fixed first — the predators, then the institutions — before the general mechanism: the institutional scotoma, a blind spot that fills itself in (the optic-nerve analogy — the blindness is real AND the sense of seeing everything is real); the second client (community cohesion displacing the child); euphemism as machinery, not politeness — file "raped children" as "risky choices" and the institution manages the child's behavior instead of arresting the men. The diagnostic: "a starved institution fails at random; a corrupted one fails in a pattern." An honorable taboo mutated into disease — "do not blame the innocent" slid into "do not describe a guilty pattern if the description might embarrass a minority" — keeping only half of anti-racism's two-part principle. The suppression ledger: hiding a fact denies it to everyone who would use it well, and manufactured the extremism it claimed to prevent. Precision cuts both ways: collective indictment of Muslims is the mirror error; name the pattern at the right resolution. And the generalizing close: every society has forbidden patterns — the scotoma can form in medicine, finance, universities, intelligence, churches, families. Attention is moral infrastructure.
