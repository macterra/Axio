**Thesis.** Cultural evolution is not hill-climbing on a fixed fitness landscape: values themselves define what counts as fitness, so the terrain deforms as cultures move — general relativity, not Newtonian optimization; culture is the mass-energy, the fitness function the curvature, evolution the geodesic motion. Four consequences: Hanson's cultural drift (selection too weak to correct maladaptive value change), Wilford's accumulated loss of embodied practices, built-in relativism (no neutral vantage), and locally rational geodesics into global dead ends. At the individual scale the same geometry produces Joscha Bach's black holes of thought space: the gravity well of belief, the event horizon past which the frame dictates permissible reasoning, the singularity of absolute certainty — and the field effects that bend even adversaries' language at a distance. The escape from built-in relativism is cultural invariants — and the first candidate, agency preservation, becomes chapter 4's Criterion.
