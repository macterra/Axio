**Thesis.** A two-pole taxonomy of pathological patterns. Mind viruses arrive wearing angel's wings: unimpeachable ideals camouflage structural rot, dissent gets moralized (making resistance framable as corruption), institutions get captured, and the stated values invert — equality produces new elites, inclusion produces division; their memetic fitness is rhetorical, not practical ("they win debates, not wars"). Destructor memes are the opposite pole: no camouflage, just the glamor of annihilation — negation marketed as authenticity, "intensity in place of meaning, fire in place of light," parasitizing the emotional circuits of genuine revolution. The antidotes differ accordingly: relentless empiricism against the noble-faced virus (judge by results; when ideals demand bureaucracy to enforce them, betrayal will follow), and Phosphorism — life, creation, agency, peace as creative space — against the nihilist meme, because despair is a pathogen, not a revelation.
