**Thesis.** Labels do two kinds of covert work. The motte-and-bailey label runs an oscillation: "Islamophobia" holds a defensible core (prejudice against Muslims as people is wrong — the motte) that provides cover for the expansive use (branding criticism of doctrine as bigotry — the bailey); challenged, accusers retreat to the motte while accusations in practice target doctrine critics, creating a double bind (accept the charge and self-censor, or reject it and appear to defend bigotry) — and diluting the term for the genuine prejudice it should name. The line: people deserve protection from persecution; ideas do not deserve protection from critique. The shibboleth runs a membership test: a vocabulary that encodes loyalty and ideological assumptions (from "late-stage capitalism" through "lived experience" to "degrowth"), whose decoding is a navigational skill — the words reveal group commitments more reliably than they describe the world.
