**Thesis.** The mature "Against" pair, plus the history they run on. Land acknowledgments invoke the moral vocabulary of theft while refusing every costly implication — "confession before the catering": announcing an injury while keeping the benefit converts material claims into linguistic ones (ownership→"presence," restitution→"awareness"), and the first-step defense fails its own test (a first step deserves the name only if attached to a second — a budget line, a land transfer, rent); the honest version would be "we believe this land was taken unjustly, and we intend to keep it." Race-conditional remedies commit the complementary error: the racialized-only job posting reproduces the mechanism of racism — race as an eligibility condition — under critique's own vocabulary; motive and statutory permission don't change what the rule does; once conditional racial preference is admitted, "the institution is no longer against racial discrimination as such — it is against unauthorized racial discrimination." A remedy should target the mechanism producing the harm, not convert the demographic correlate into the allocation rule. Beneath both sits instrumentalized history: the widespread belief that "white people invented slavery" — a product of curricular compression, activist narrative fill, and tribal epistemics, against the record (slavery universal; the West uniquely its worldwide abolisher). And the foil that keeps the chapter honest: the case for "West Asia" — linguistic reform justified by accuracy is legitimate; linguistic ritual substituting for material remedy is not.
