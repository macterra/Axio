**Thesis.** Some true facts are radioactive — but the hazard usually lives in the misframing, not the facts. The HBD infohazard is the type case: replace the universal-scalar model of intelligence with the games framework and the poison drains away — populations differ as strategy-context matches, not as ranks on one ladder; the guardrails are context-dependence, the agency principle (individuals play outside ancestral niches), and no universal scalar. The method claim is containment versus suppression: neutralize the interpretive hazard, keep the facts — because suppression's costs get priced fully in chapter 21. And radioactive content needs carriers: contrarianism decomposes into four strategies with sharply different risk profiles — taboo-flipping, provocation-laundering (which doesn't launder; it keeps the provocation in circulation), cynical nihilism, and the ironist whose mask "works for years, but eventually it slips."
