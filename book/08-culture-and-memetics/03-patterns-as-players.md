**Thesis.** The volume's theoretical anchor. Patterns — "any coherent configuration of information that remains stable enough to exert influence" — not people, are the real units of cultural selection. Behavioral, cognitive, institutional, cultural, technological, economic: coherence, not scale, defines them. Survival equals internal coherence plus fit with the incentive environment, and the environment rewards performance, not virtue or truth; patterns persist beyond their hosts (procedures, doctrines, outrage cycles), and incentives filter patterns, not intentions — prestige selects for novelty, media for attention, politics for loyalty, markets for growth. The pathologies have names: pattern drift, pattern lock, pattern collapse. And the threshold insight: "You do not operate patterns; patterns operate through you."
