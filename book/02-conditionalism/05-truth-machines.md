**Thesis.** The formal history of logic converges on Conditionalism: Leibniz's necessary/contingent divide collapses into a continuum of conditional stability, and Tarski, Gödel, Turing, and Chaitin show no system carries its own semantics — truth is a property of evaluations, not statements.
