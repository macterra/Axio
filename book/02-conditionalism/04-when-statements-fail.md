**Thesis.** Truth arises only when a statement's conditions are bound: the six-part taxonomy of underdetermination (unbound indexicals, failed reference, unbound quantifiers, hidden antecedents, standardless evaluations, category errors) explains why many well-formed sentences are neither true nor false, and gives a principled definition of nonsense.
