**Thesis.** The Part III capstone, from the volume's most mature speech statement (Jun 2026). Free speech is tested only by speech the powerful find reprehensible — a platform with nothing you find reprehensible has already removed the hard case. The epistemic core: no institution knows enough to pre-sort which condemned opinions are early signals of false consensus, and there is no court of final appeal, so the dispute must run in public. The architecture: protect lawful opinion categorically; push filtering to the user ("'I do not want to see this' concerns my attention; 'no one may say this' reaches for authority over everyone else"); separate publication from promotion (no right to forced amplification, but hiding censorship in ranking is worse than removal); enforce only a narrow conduct rule — doxxing, impersonation, malware, botnets, credible threats, explicit coercive coordination — the acts that defeat user agency or attack the infrastructure itself.

**Sources.** free-speech-is-civilizational-infrastructure, essentially standalone: the useful mess, safety-as-sanitation critique, the advertiser trap (the engagement machine manufactures the arousal it then hires moderators to sanitize), the mob line (explicit weaponization of an audience, not downstream-hostility speculation), and the remedy — many clients, many filters, portable identity, protocol neutrality: no moral landlord for the network.
