**Thesis.** The map chapter. Replace the left–right spectrum — a representational failure whose artifacts include the endless Nazi-"socialism" dispute — with a multi-dimensional phase space (command, ownership, beneficiaries, and the missing variable: evaluability), within which Axio defines an admissible region: the set of political orders compatible with surviving agency. The conditions: authority traceable to delegation; contestation without violence; participation not enforced by unendorsed threat; obligations legible in advance; exit without destruction of the capacity to act — where exit is disqualified only by credible threats of imposed harm, not ordinary relocation costs. The case sweep cashes it out empirically: Nazi Germany fails everything; the USSR and PRC fail on evaluability regardless of economic flexibility; the USA is a boundary case; Switzerland sits comfortably inside on structure rather than virtue; Singapore is admissible but brittle; and voluntary systems can drift out when network effects make exit nominal.

**Sources.** politics-as-phase-space (Jan 2026, standalone; secondary Vol 4 — the Axionic program applied to political philosophy): everything above, plus delegability/contestability/legibility vocabulary and the punchline that reframes the volume: "The future of politics is not left or right. It is inside or outside the region where agency remains viable."
