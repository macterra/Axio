**Thesis.** Frame first: total crime elimination would require agency-destroying surveillance and control — society itself becoming "the most comprehensive destroyer of human agency" — so the coherent aim is maximizing total agency, enforcing until marginal agency gained equals marginal agency lost. Within that frame, "paying a debt to society" is moral incoherence: society is not the injured party, and punishment divorced from restitution severs action from consequence. The positive system is Universal Compensatory Justice: layered, insurance-based compensation restoring victims immediately and automatically, cost recovery migrating to the risk originator by subrogation, behavior shaped by risk-based premiums instead of prisons — justice as restoration, deterrence as price.
