**Thesis.** Immigration restrictions are a form of harm — coercive reductions of agency on both sides of the border: the migrant's blocked revealed preference and the host's denied freedom of association. The cultural-threat objection dissolves under the agency test: difference and demographic shift are not harm; only demonstrably coercive actors justify (selective, not blanket) filtering. Colonization was condemnable because movement was coercive, not because people moved — so "immigration equals colonization" is equivocation; and justice for historical colonization means agency restoration through voluntary means, not coercive redistribution from uninvolved descendants, which replicates the original wrong. The demographic-anxiety coda: fertility decline creates no personal obligation and no license for coercion — if voluntary incentives can't reverse it, adaptation (including openness) is the legitimate response.
