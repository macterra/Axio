**Thesis.** Classical liberalism is chronically unpopular despite its demonstrated benefits because its principles run against evolved cognition and political incentive: spontaneous order is counterintuitive, benefits are dispersed while grievances concentrate, politics rewards centralizers, narratives want heroes, and liberalism "offers no comforting mythologies, no simplistic scapegoats, no charismatic leaders promising salvation." The equilibrium it loses to is mapped by the political triangle — Individualism, Maternalism (welfare/support), Paternalism (order/protection) — whose center produces citizens in permanent adolescence: sheltered enough to be dependent, regulated enough to avoid full responsibility, never truly exercising self-determination.
