**Thesis.** The inference from disparity to injury is invalid: harm requires a mechanism — coercion, fraud, capture, exclusion — and envy is the political psychology that skips the causal work and proceeds to punishment. Two movements. Psychology and power: status comparison is local (parents, peers, the despised Other), rank-violation envy is its ugliest form, and merging entrepreneur with rent-seeker into one hated class protects actual predators — attacking wealth as such destroys the tools for identifying illegitimate wealth. Statics and mobility: inequality is a snapshot description, harm is blocked transition; distress tracks foreclosed possibility, not Gini — "a society that preserves mobility can tolerate inequality; a society that suppresses mobility cannot tolerate even perfect equality."

**Sources.** against-envy (May 2026, canonical): the mechanism test, the concessions engaged on their premises (Rawlsian, utilitarian, stability), the honest hard problem (extreme inequality hardening into coercive control — moats, capture, chokepoints — with the boundary questions), and the constructive program (dismantle licensing cartels, zoning, credential cartels, asset inflation, corporate welfare). against-moralizing-distributions (Jan 2026): the Nature meta-analysis + Taleb hook (168 studies, 11M people, no reliable inequality–well-being link), the causal reversal (coercive institutions freeze ladders; inequality is the symptom), competitive disadvantage ≠ coercion. the-politics-of-envy: flavor only — "primate comparator loop," destructor logic, the condemnation register.
