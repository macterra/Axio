**Thesis.** Compassion cannot launder coercion: harm is in the method, not the motive, and "net good" aggregation cannot justify harming one group to benefit another. Two case studies. UBI: the funding mechanism, not the program's aims, determines its ethical status — voluntarily funded UBI is unobjectionable; tax- or inflation-funded UBI imposes values society demonstrably doesn't hold (the revealed-preference test). Foreign aid: when 90% is siphoned to oppressors, funding the pipeline is complicity — Bastiat's seen and unseen applied to the aid economy, where visible short-term losses hide invisible systemic harm.
