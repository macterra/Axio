A man empties his neighbor's safe and donates every dollar to a food bank. The families are really fed. At trial he argues that his compassionate motive and the net good erase the theft. No court accepts the defense: the theft and gift are two acts, and the second does not unmake the first.

Enlarge the scale, route the taking through a legislature, and swap the crowbar for a tax code, and the rejected defense becomes standard. The appeal points to the food bank, never the safe. Does the method of redistribution itself cause harm?

## The Method, Not the Motive

Coercive redistribution is enforced through threats of fines, liens, seizure, and imprisonment. Those sanctions burden the taxpayer's agency; collection and enforcement can impose further setbacks. Whether the effects are wrongful remains a justification question that compassionate purpose cannot settle in advance.

Motives are facts about the coercer; harm is a fact about the coerced. A threat backed by prison does not change mechanism when its proceeds buy vaccines rather than villas. Kindness at the destination does not launder coercion at the source.

Aggregation then weighs the taxpayer's loss against the recipient's benefit and calls the result “net good.” [Against Utilitarianism](../05-value-and-ethics/14-against-utilitarianism.md) rejects that interpersonal ledger: one agent's contracted futures are not paid off by another's expanded ones. **It is not compassionate to fund charity through extortion.**

[Agency, Not Equality](17-agency-not-equality.md) established the target; [Needs, Conflicts, and Hard Cases](07-needs-conflicts-and-hard-cases.md) established that need alone identifies no debtor. The remaining method question appears at both ends of the pipeline: how funds enter and where they go.

## Basic Income, Two Ways

Universal Basic Income cleanly tests intake because it is cash rather than a service. Its appeal is genuine: alleviate poverty, provide a floor, free people to pursue worthwhile work. Its ethical status still depends on how it is funded.

A UBI funded by donations, mutual-aid societies, foundations, or opt-in insurance pools is compassion at institutional scale. Build it and I will cheer.

A UBI funded by taxation or money creation is a different object. Applying [the funding test](04-extortion-funded-organizations.md) means asking how revenue arrives and what happens to those who decline to send it. A coerced UBI expands recipients' futures by contracting funders' futures under threat.

Tax receipts do not show that each payer values UBI at its cost; voluntary contributions would be positive evidence. Yet voluntary underfunding can reflect free-riding, assurance problems, liquidity, or disagreement rather than lack of value, as [The Myth of Underprovision](../06-markets-and-money/08-the-myth-of-underprovision.md) explains. Advocates must identify the coordination failure, compare mechanisms, and meet the Grey Zone's burden.

## Aid That Funds the Oppressor

Outflow can corrupt even honestly raised money. Imagine a foreign-aid NGO whose audit finds 90% of its budget funding a dictator's cronies and security services while 10% delivers life-saving aid. When the budget is cut, the objection is that *cutting this aid is essentially killing people*.

The factual kernel is real: clinics may close and people may die. But the objection counts visible losses while ignoring the strengthened secret police, entrenched regime, closed newspaper, and foreclosed futures financed by the other 90%. The 10% helps its recipients; the 90% helps contract agency across a society.

If a humanitarian organization knowingly handed most of its cash to an armed gang, the fact that some gang spending fed families would not erase the complicity. Bureaucratizing the handoff changes nothing that matters.

Cutting corrupted aid is therefore not “essentially killing people.” Withholding a gift does not invade its hoped-for recipient, while continuing the pipeline positively funds coercion. It is the burglar's ledger with a logistics department.

The alternative is verified conditional aid, direct transfers that bypass regime intermediaries, and institution-building that makes communities harder to oppress. As [Borders and Movement](16-borders-and-movement.md) argued, agency-enhancing help widens futures without feeding their enemies. Compassion requires caring more about the outcome than the gesture.

## The Question That Remains

Trace the money rather than the mission statement. Compassion can be corrupted at intake by coercive collection or at outflow by funding coercers; pointing at beneficiaries does not answer scrutiny of either method.

Classification does not by itself establish realized injury, wrongfulness, or authority. Funding formulas, democratic ratification, and purpose can matter to justification without changing the mechanism. Whether a pension or public school can survive the resulting burden is the question [Against Socialism](20-against-socialism.md) takes up.

What classification forbids is laundering. Argue for compulsory transfer as coercion carrying its burden, not as generosity, solidarity, or love. The corruption begins when the safe is opened with a crowbar and the receipt made out to charity.
