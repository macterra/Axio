**Thesis.** Rights are not discovered in nature, granted by God, or created by institutions — they are forged: born in agency, made real by enforcement. But force alone is not enough ("slaveholders had preferences"): a right is a defensible boundary around agency whose justifying principle survives reciprocal application to the coercer — with the Anti-Evasion Rule barring self-exempting definitions of who counts. From reciprocity the classical liberal rights list (body, property, speech, privacy, association, due process) falls out as the conditions under which agency survives conflict; and when enforcement is monopolized by a state that can seize property, destroy livelihood, and criminalize resistance, rights are revealed as conditional permissions — parchment barriers defended only by lived resistance.
