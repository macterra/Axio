**Thesis.** Liberal democracies do not choose thought-policing; they drift into it through three linked category errors — thought as action's precursor, offense as harm, prevention as justification — propagated rationally through risk-averse bureaucracies where forwarding suspicion is safe and discounting it is risky. The result is soft totalitarianism: coercion experienced as ordinary administration. Two accelerants: declaring policies "not up for debate" (conceptual smuggling that elevates interpretations to sacred values, removing the nonviolent mechanism for resolving disagreement), and the game-theoretic temptation — retaliatory censorship as a "stabilizing equilibrium" — which legitimizes coercion as a political tactic and cannot be walked back.
