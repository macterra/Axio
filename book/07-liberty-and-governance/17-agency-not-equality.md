**Thesis.** Justice should be measured by agency, not equality: inequality per se harms no one; poverty — absolute deprivation of agency — is the real problem. The luck argument fails at the joint: even if success is largely due to unchosen genes and upbringing, the origin of someone's advantages does not change the moral status of coercing them. And "equal opportunity" — the egalitarianism libertarians permit themselves — is incoherent: opportunities are downstream of prior outcomes, so equalizing opportunity requires coercively equalizing outcomes; the ideal must be dropped, not moderated. Abandon equality as a goal entirely and target agency.
