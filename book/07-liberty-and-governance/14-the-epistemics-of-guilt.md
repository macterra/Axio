**Thesis.** Adjudication is applied epistemology. The presumption of innocence and trial process encode epistemic humility into the state's use of violence — accusation is not truth, intelligence is inference, and killing on suspicion forecloses every future in which innocence would have been revealed. "The essence of civilization is not that we punish the guilty, but that we refuse to kill the possibly innocent." But honoring the principle does not require preserving its current implementation: the jury system prioritizes democratic symbolism over epistemic rigor — voir dire filters out expertise, verdicts carry no accountability, rhetoric beats evidence — and better error-filtering machinery is available: professional epistemic judges, AI decision support (assist, never decide), prediction markets aggregating expert credences.
