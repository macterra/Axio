**Thesis.** Coordination has intrinsic costs that rise with scale; past a threshold, large coordinating structures lose evaluability — interpretation migrates into the structure, responsibility diffuses — and become Leviathans: mechanisms that act, enforce, and optimize without being agents. The deepest indictment of the state is not that it is evil but that it is not an agent, and the collapse is largely irreversible. What remains inside the viable region is the Dominion architecture: federated virtual jurisdictions with sovereign opt-in, expulsion-only enforcement, exit supremacy, asset portability, and capability isolation — "separate airtight vessels rather than a shared room," aggression made infeasible rather than prohibited, drift handled by mobility rather than reform: localize coordination, globalize exit.

**Sources.** against-leviathan (Dec 2025): the dose-response honesty (small coalitions strengthen agency; the same mechanisms later destroy it), the thermodynamic register, the alignment implication ("alignment is downstream of agency"). dominions (Dec 2025): the causal chain (operator-controlled assets → rising exit costs → captivity → sacrifice gradients → collapse — hence asset portability is constitutive), freedom density, the scope honesty (digital governance only; physical scarcity is ch 35's problem), the Nozick lineage acknowledged and re-grounded.
