**Thesis.** The Part VI capstone. Centrism, properly understood, is not tepid moderation but a governance discipline for evolving ecological systems under uncertainty and irreversibility: society is path-dependent and cascade-prone, so power is hazardous, certainty is brittle, and agency is the condition under which correction remains possible. Its moral compass is the variance distinction — voluntary divergence is protected, coercively produced variance is not — yielding selective intolerance of coercion rather than complicity. It licenses rupture where stability rests on coercive agency-suppression (abolition, civil rights) and decisiveness where delay compounds irreversible harm, answering the passivity charge. It provokes hostility because it denies every faction's moral monopoly and refuses narrative compression — the aesthetics of boredom.
