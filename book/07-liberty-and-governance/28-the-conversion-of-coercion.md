**Thesis.** Part VII opens by naming the exit. "Everything is politics" equivocates between coercive power-struggle and voluntary coordination; politics is not a law of nature but an exploit — enabled by shared resources, information asymmetry, and enforceable leverage — and it collapses where asymmetries are neutralized. Civilization, viewed as an evolutionary process, is the long conversion of coercion into coordination: institutionalized cooperation, expanding moral circles, error-correction machinery (science, markets, free speech), and the steady replacement of politics with protocols. The micro-foundation is principled behavior itself: acting on principle is strategically optimal, not merely noble — predictability enhances others' agency, lowers transaction friction, and builds the trust that reduces the need for enforcement.
