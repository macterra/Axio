**Thesis.** A lineage chapter: Mill's harm principle founded the liberal tradition but left "harm" undefined; the Non-Aggression Principle inherited the ambiguity plus circularity (aggression = illegitimate coercion) and a hidden dependence on presupposed property rights; the Agency Protection Principle repairs both — "coercion is justified if, and only if, it prevents or remedies violations of voluntary agency." Agency (liberty + capability + opportunity) replaces liberty as the fundamental political goal: it subsumes liberty and makes trade-offs explicit instead of masking them.

**Sources.** on-liberty (harm-principle half only — the free-discussion half feeds ch 8): Mill's twin threats (state + social tyranny), harm as concrete/demonstrable/non-trivial. the-agency-protection-principle: the NAP three-defect critique (keeper) and the APP biconditional. upgrading-liberty: the agency definition, ΔNetAgency test, applications (environmental regulation can pass; most immigration restrictions fail). defending-agency: the four-objection FAQ (utilitarianism-in-disguise, rights-ignoring, unquantifiability, authoritarian-enabling) as the chapter's closing section.
