**Thesis.** Socialism's core move is converting need into jurisdiction — treating human need as enforceable title to others' labor and property. The premise itself must be refused: need creates opportunity (for aid, invention, association), not title; obligation requires a bridge (causation, consent, contract, guardianship, fiduciary duty), and need alone leaves the span unbuilt. The definitional discipline cuts both ways: socialism = the coercive socialization of a function, which makes public schools, hospitals, and pensions socialism by dose — and the danger climbs with dose, domain, monopoly, exit, and feedback, not by prophecy. Production precedes distribution ("socialism asks who gets the bread; it rarely asks who keeps the bread coming"); scale manufactures ignorance; and the deepest cost is moral displacement — a society of clients, taxpayers, and administrators where neighbours and benefactors used to be.
