**Thesis.** Authoritarianism is humanity's psychological default — the product of evolved hierarchy, cognitive outsourcing, conformity bias, craving for certainty, weaponized fear, and the trade of agency for comfort — while liberalism is a fragile, effortful cultural achievement requiring continuous defense. The dynamics follow the psychology: factions champion liberal principles instrumentally while out of power and predictably abandon them upon gaining it — free speech becomes censorship, limited government becomes security authoritarianism — so a stable liberal order requires actors who hold principles as terminal values. Definitionally, the opposition is three-axis: authority vs autonomy, coercion vs consent, dogmatic certainty vs conditional epistemology; both sides want order — liberalism gets it voluntarily, and upholds rule OF law against rule BY law.
