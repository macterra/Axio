**Thesis.** "No one is above the law" is myth: law is a coordination technology that binds only where power is roughly balanced — "a gravity field that weakens with altitude." Hegemon impunity is a structural position, not an anomaly; the same conduct that triggers process for a small state triggers nothing for a great one, so identity of actor, not conduct, determines legal consequence. Meanwhile, at the other end, much law is written for the worst-case few: both tails — the chronic offender AND the elite malefactor — drive rule design, and the compliant middle pays in surrendered freedom. The civic question is how much liberty the majority should give up to contain the tails; the remedy for the top tail is architectural, not moral: systems where reciprocal constraint operates as inevitability, not aspiration.
