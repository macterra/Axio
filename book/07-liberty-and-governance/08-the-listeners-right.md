**Thesis.** Free speech is fundamentally the listener's right: every act of censorship is coercion primarily against the audience — a denial of their intellectual autonomy that infantilizes them and drives dangerous ideas underground where challenge cannot reach. Thought and speech are not separate freedoms but one — cognitive freedom — because thinking informs speaking and speaking tests thinking ("to forbid speech is to starve thought"). And the popular legalist frame (xkcd 1357: "free speech just means the government can't arrest you") confuses statute with substance: free speech is conditional freedom from coercion — of any kind, state or private — to express criticism; renaming employer threats and mob pressure "consequences" is laundering coercion.
