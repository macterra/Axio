**Thesis.** Political vocabulary has been inflated into uselessness, and the remedy is taxonomy plus discipline. "Socialism" names three radically distinct systems — National Socialism (ethno-authoritarian), International Socialism (class authoritarian), Liberal Socialism (democratic welfare capitalism) — and the structural axis (authoritarian vs liberal) matters more than the left–right content: the first two converge despite ideological rivalry. "Fascist" has undergone the same inflation; Doyle's razor restores a sharp diagnostic — condoning the murder of political opponents — that indicts the celebratory fringe without smearing the mainstream. "When everyone is a fascist, the label becomes meaningless."
