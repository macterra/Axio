**Thesis.** Yudkowsky and Soares' eight cruxes, taken seriously: the naive-build default does trend toward doom — but doom is policy-endogenous, not fated, and disaggregating naive-build from governed-build yields a concrete stack: architecture-agnostic controls, tripwire governance, mechanism-design chokepoints. What it must not yield: the authoritarian roadmap of hardware surveillance and centralized compute, whose cure is worse than the disease.
