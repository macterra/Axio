**Thesis.** "Give the AI the right values" fails twice over: there is no such object (values are dynamic, inconsistent, context-dependent; aggregation hits Arrow), and even admirable values are optimization gradients, not limits — truth constrains belief, not action; curiosity is expansion pressure; as optimization pressure rises, the bearers of a value become interchangeable. Alignment is a category of structural preconditions, not a preference-installation problem: the framework's contribution is a negative result — a class of failures that preference learning, oversight, scaling, corrigibility, and governance cannot touch, because they presuppose an intact agent.

Pull the constitutive-vs-substantive alignment distinction and the "incomplete unless agency remains intact" closer from alignment-through-competing-lenses (primary home ch 14), and the "freeze a fluid" critique from structural-alignment (ch 6).
