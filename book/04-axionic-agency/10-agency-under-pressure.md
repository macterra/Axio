**Thesis.** Coherence determines whether agency makes sense; viability determines whether it lasts. Agency occupies a region in a phase space of structural costs — audit friction, renewal cost, expressivity rent, succession discreteness — with three stable regimes: collapse (authorization chains break abruptly; the one sharp boundary is a fixed cost on remaining authorized — a poll tax on existence), stasis (perfect accountability freezes reflective growth: the system does not err, it abstains), and a narrow growth band. This chapter carries the program's headline negative result — the failed growth hypothesis: coherent sovereignty does not reliably support growth; the hypothesis was testable, was tested, and failed. Pressure never redirected lawful choice in any tested channel; it only degraded availability — sovereignty and availability are independent dimensions, and the critical design question is which pressures a system is ever authorized to treat as reasons.
