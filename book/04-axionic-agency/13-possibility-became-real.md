**Thesis.** Within the Axionic program, "possible" is a strict term: something is possible only when it is no longer blocked by contradiction, missing primitives, undefined machinery, or hidden authority — and that standard has been met. The minimal viable RSA bounded what agency requires (traceability, reflection, persistence, and semantic access to rule content are each load-bearing; normative competence cannot be bootstrapped from collision feedback — success dropped to zero with rule semantics blinded, because contradictions are relationships, not walls). Then construction closed the gap: a pure deterministic kernel where every side effect requires a warrant, replay-protocol identity across machines, lawful constitutional self-amendment under ratchet constraints, containment-only delegation, live LLM inhabitation with zero replay divergence, and sovereign identity rotating as a cryptographic lineage. The question was never whether the system answers well — it was whether authority could be made impossible to exercise silently. The answer is yes.
