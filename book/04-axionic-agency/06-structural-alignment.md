**Thesis.** Goal meanings do not survive intelligence, so alignment must be redefined as staying within an interpretation-preserving semantic phase: learning may add detail (Refinement Symmetry) but must not add new ways to dissolve distinctions that previously mattered, and new "good" states must descend from old ones (Anti-Trivialization). The alignment target is an equivalence class of learning trajectories, not a goal — alignment is a boundary condition, not a training objective ("once agency leaves the agency-preserving region, the game is over"). Closing the framework requires six structural obligations plus a Bridge/Relaxation layer over probabilistic substrates — which mandates hybrid architectures, because no total end-to-end evaluator can express undefinedness at any scale.
