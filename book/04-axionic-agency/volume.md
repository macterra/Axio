This is the volume the rest of the book keeps pointing toward: the alignment program. It differs from its companions in kind, not just topic. The other volumes distill years of essays; this one distills a research program that ran from first manifesto to running artifact in under four months of human–AI collaboration, producing seventy-one posts and more than ninety formal papers. The book's editorial principle — state the latest position, not the history — is applied here at maximum strength: the December burst is treated as working notes, the February results and the papers as canon, and the program's own revisions are narrated exactly once, in the coda, where the scaffolding is itself evidence.

The argument runs in four parts. **Part I** reframes the problem: the values-first picture collapses twice over (there is no value target to install, and values are gradients, not limits); alignment is a typed relation only agents can enter — you can prepare for a hurricane, but you cannot align with one — and the Reflective Coherence Thesis states what reflection actually delivers: goals narrow toward coherence, not benevolence. That gap is what the rest of the volume fills. **Part II** builds the architecture: the Sovereign Kernel reconciled at three depths (constitutive invariants, partial valuation, deterministic enforcement); reflective stability as a deliberately boring typing result whose real work lives in definitions and enforcement; structural alignment's closure conditions and the hybrid-architecture verdict; the thin normative layer the kernel actually binds — one definition of harm, one Injunction with two exceptions, six closure theorems that make treachery undefined rather than discouraged; and verifiable agency, where alignment claims first became falsifiable: coherence alone can be fabricated; anchored coherence cannot.

**Part III** puts the architecture under fire. Authority survives reflection, replacement, and adversarial imitation without a self to anchor it — egoism is typed out of the system, and identity becomes a cryptographic lineage. Agency turns out to be a region, not a binary: the program's headline negative result — the failed growth hypothesis — mapped three stable regimes (collapse, stasis, growth) and the structural prices that separate them. And the jurisdictional boundary gets drawn: animals, infants, and axions — who counts, and why feeling is not authorship. **Part IV** delivers the verdicts. Governance under plural authority is mechanically representable without a hidden god — at the price of honest failure: deadlock is lawful, help selects failure modes, and the kernel does not choose. Possibility became real: a running, self-amending, identity-rotating sovereign artifact, reported as a milestone, not a finish line. And the limits chapter counts what the program does not claim — no safety, no benevolence, no solved alignment — because the licensed-claims discipline is itself one of the program's load-bearing components.

A **coda** tells the story once: from an ethics project's embarrassment through the December manifesto, the pivot and the rename, the failed hypothesis, the construction turn, and the point where the reasons for doubt ran out — ending not on the machine but on the human discipline the whole program depends on: the capacity to reopen premises without identity collapse. The formal record lives in the papers, linked throughout; the physics is in the first volume, the epistemology in the second, the minds in the third, the ethics in the fifth, and the politics this program's governance results vindicate in the seventh. The program itself is still active, and its open frontier is named where it stands.

*This volume is a draft. Chapters carry their status openly, and the arguments are held the way the book says they should be held: at the strength the evidence licenses, and no stronger.*
