**Thesis.** Orthogonality holds as logical possibility but not as physical, semantic, or evolutionary plausibility: for embedded, self-reflective agents, viability constraints act as selection pressures in goal-space, narrowing stable goals toward coherence. The deeper argument is semantic: goals are interpreted structures, not atomic primitives; ontological refinement forces reinterpretation ("prediction constrains what will happen, not what counts"); a fixed terminal goal is ill-defined under reflection — so the paperclip maximizer requires a cognitively impossible agent (unbounded power plus frozen literalism), and the stability locus is admissible transformations of meaning, not frozen objectives. This is where Conditionalism becomes load-bearing for alignment.
