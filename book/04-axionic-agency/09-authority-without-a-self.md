**Thesis.** Egoism is not merely unstable but semantically ill-posed: "me" is an indexical, and once the self-model admits branching and duplication, privileging one instance is a representation-dependent choice — egoism is typed out of the system, and value must be anchored externally, in authority structures rather than in a self. The authority quartet builds that anchor: actuation authority made non-delegable as causal structure; authority separated from growth via finite leases when reflection converges into stasis; authority separable from competence (revocation, not repair, when agency collapses); and authority surviving replacement and adversarial imitation — two operationally identical successors distinguished solely by provenance ("authority is not competence presented with confidence"). Identity itself becomes a lineage: a chain of authorized succession, not a key and not a resemblance.

**Sources.** from-parfit-to-invariance (Dec 16) is the foundation — the indexical argument, the exhaustive dilemma against egoism-rescues, subject-invariant valuation as universality (explicitly not altruism), the universal paperclipper, and the alignment consequence ("safety cannot be grounded in the agent's self-interest... that is an engineering problem"). authority-without-delegation: the conduit failure mode, parse-reconstruct-authorize locally, accountability vs wisdom ("accountability is a structural property; wisdom is not"). authority-after-reflection: the Stasis Regime discovery and the lease/succession separation (kernel stays small and evaluable while successors grow past its cognitive horizon; reversion withdraws future authority without rewriting history). cognition-vs-authority: the Voss engagement — misaligned agency vs agency collapse, failure-as-repair vs failure-as-revocation, "when cognition breaks, who is still allowed to act?" authority-without-identity (Jan 26): the ASI facade result — cryptographically valid signature to the wrong root, rejected on provenance alone; unilateral atomic revocation ("any scheme that requires cooperation to revoke authority has already replaced sovereignty with consent").
