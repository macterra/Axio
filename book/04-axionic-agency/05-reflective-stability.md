**Thesis.** The Vingean impossibility — you cannot predict a smarter successor — dissolves once authorization shifts from anticipated behavior to structural admissibility: a sovereign agent does not authorize behaviors, it authorizes transformations of itself, judged solely by kernel preservation. The Reflective Stability Theorem is deliberately boring ("well-typed programs do not go wrong"): if an agent can only evaluate kernel-preserving modifications, it never chooses a kernel-destroying one — all the work lives in the definitions and the enforcement. Self-modification divides exhaustively: goals, strategies, world-models, and substrate may all change ("a reflective agent may change anything except the structures that make change meaningful"); identity severance, counterfactual collapse, preference freezing, and permanent delegation to non-reflective processes are non-denoting.

**Sources.** beyond-vingean-reflection (Dec 21) is the canonical register — explicit conditionality (kernel non-simulability as a load-bearing, unproven premise), the complexity ceiling (unverifiable transformations inadmissible; stasis preferred to self-corruption), non-denotation blocking Löbian traps ("the agent is never asked to prove that a future agent is safe"), the physical-failure carve-out (Rowhammer is a security failure, not an alignment failure). the-reflective-stability-theorem (Dec 13) supplies the intuition and the proof sketch (evaluative machinery annihilating itself mid-evaluation) — write it in the later hedged register; "theorem" survives as a typing result, not a safety proof. boundary-conditions-for-selfmodification supplies the permitted/prohibited taxonomy and recursive reflectivity (no handing ultimate control to a non-reflective sub-process).
