**Thesis.** Axionic Ethics is a new genus: grounded in agency, with one invariant — no coercive harm against innocents. Against utilitarianism it refuses aggregation; against deontology, universal law; against virtue ethics, character-first; against contractualism, hypothetical consent; against egoism, unbounded partiality; against libertarianism, natural-rights metaphysics. 'Why be moral?' dissolves: coexistence is a domain, not a duty, and coercers exit it.
