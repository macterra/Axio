**Thesis.** Harm is the non-consensual degradation of an agent's capacity to pursue its valued goals — which excludes offense, rejection, and envy, and blocks moral inflation. Evil is intentional harm; dangerous is capability without intent; menace is both. And comfort is not care: indulging delusion constrains agency and betrays the agent it claims to protect.
