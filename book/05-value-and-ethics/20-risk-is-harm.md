**Thesis.** Harm is the reduction of an agent's viable futures, and imposed risk is its probabilistic form — harm at the moment of decision, eliminating moral luck. In the QBU, probability is Measure, so risk imposition literally shifts an agent's future-measure toward negative outcomes; coercive harm is formalized as divergence from the agent's uncoerced baseline.
