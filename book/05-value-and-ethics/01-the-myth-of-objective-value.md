**Thesis.** All value is subjective: it exists only in relation to agents capable of wanting, choosing, and sacrificing. Morality, being made of value judgments, cannot be objective — every proposed grounding (God, reason, evolution, intuition) smuggles in someone's preferences. The death of objective morality is the birth of moral agency.
