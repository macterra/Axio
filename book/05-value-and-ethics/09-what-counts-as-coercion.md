**Thesis.** Coercion is the credible threat of actual harm to gain compliance — four elements, each tested by counterexample. Credibility is agent-relative (a toy-gun mugging still coerces, because the victim's Credence is rationally high), and violence differs in kind: force collapses agency where coercion revalues the choice landscape.
