**Thesis.** Aggregation makes persons fungible; demandingness mortgages every life; coercion becomes a ledger cost; impartiality erases situated agents; 'suffering as universal currency' is moral realism by stealth; and population ethics generates repugnant conclusions structurally. Utilitarianism and agency-centered ethics are rival conceptions of what ethics is about.
