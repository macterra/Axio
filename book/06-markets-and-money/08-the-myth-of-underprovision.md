**Thesis.** The textbook claim that markets underprovide public goods is a model output, not an empirical finding — its assumptions bake in the state's necessity, and 'underprovision' means only 'less than a planner's social welfare function demands.' History (private lighthouses, turnpikes, subscription schools) and technology (micropayments, cryptographic exclusion) say otherwise.
