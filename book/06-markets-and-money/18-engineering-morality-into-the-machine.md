**Thesis.** Institutions follow incentives, so morality must be engineered into the machine: Canada's MAID drifts toward death because death is cheaper — the fix is a 10-point incentive protocol, not rhetoric. And legalization debates commit the regulatory-counterfactual fallacy: markets reveal preferences, harm must be defined as agency-reduction, and the answer to engineered traps is agency-preserving constraint, not prohibition.
